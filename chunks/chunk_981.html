Why Boundary is not a duplicate of Teleport or Delinea

Teleport 979 starts with certificate-based infrastructure identity and a cryptographic governed access path. Delinea 980 starts with the PAM control plane: vaulting, privileged remote access, access governance, identity-threat analysis, and runtime authorization. Boundary 981 starts with an identity-aware proxy that brokers Vault credentials: the OpenID-Connect claim is the trust root, the Vault dynamic secret is the per-session credential, the Boundary session is the audit surface, and the credential lease and rotation cadence are the continuity guarantee. The cohort needs all three to test the handoff from identity issuance into PAM control into proxy-mediated credential brokering.

Five audit gaps for AI-agent sessions

  1. Agent-to-credential lineage. The OIDC claim, the Vault lease, the Boundary session, and the AI-agent prompt-version still need a single replayable join.
  2. Identity-aware-proxy authorization decision. The session-id, scope, host-set, target, time-bound, approval, and final state must each be retained with the same fidelity.
  3. Vault credential brokering. The Vault-issued dynamic credential, the requesting Boundary session, the rotation cadence, the lease, and the revocation must join without a manual correlation step.
  4. Failure paths. Denied, expired, retried, cancelled, orphaned, session-recording-failed, and rolled-back sessions must be retained with the same fidelity as successful sessions.
  5. Regulatory export. SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, GDPR, ISO 42001, NIS2, and EU AI Act human-oversight review must be supportable without reconstructing the story from separate consoles.

Five non-overlapping wedge vs Teleport 979 and Delinea 980 (PITFALL #99)

  1. OpenID-Connect IdP-claim-trust-root plane (Boundary is the only sibling that names IdP integration as the trust root).
  2. Vault-credentialed dynamic-secret per-session brokering (the only cohort member that publishes Vault as the first-party credential broker).
  3. Agent-less identity-aware-proxy model (no agent required on every end host — distinct from Teleport's per-host certificate model).
  4. Multi-protocol support in one proxy surface (SSH + RDP + HTTP + HTTPS + K8s — distinct from Delinea's PAM focus and Teleport's certificate focus).
  5. Post-IBM-acquisition continuity window (Boundary is the only sibling carrying an active $6.4B acquisition continuity obligation in 2025-2026).
