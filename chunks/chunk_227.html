3. The 5 audit gaps documented for Agora

3.1 End-to-end per-room provenance across SD-RTN + Conversational AI + Cloud Recording + On-Premise Recording

The hardest audit question in 2026 for Agora is whether a single audit query can reconstruct `per-room-id → per-cloud-recording-id → per-LLM-call-id → per-downstream-CRM-write-id` for any channel in the last 13 months, satisfying SOC 2 CC7.2 (system operations monitoring) + HIPAA §164.312(b) (audit controls) + EU AI Act Art. 12 (record-keeping for high-risk AI systems). The 60-col join-table above is the canonical answer, but most Agora deployments ship without the cross-surface join view — they require a custom evidence-collection effort per audit cycle.

3.2 Prompt-injection + audio-poisoning defense across the Conversational AI SDK pipeline

Agora's Conversational AI SDK is the LLM-injection surface; defenses must be measured at the per-audio-frame + per-VAD-segment + per-LLM-call-message + per-TTS-segment + per-tool-call + per-cloud-recording granularity against OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt injection) + LLM03 (training-data poisoning) + LLM04 (model DoS) + LLM06 (sensitive disclosure) + LLM08 (vector/embedding poisoning) + MITRE ATLAS AML.T0051 + AML.T0054 + EU AI Act Art. 15 (accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity). The 10-column per-room-id join-table needs to be linked to per-room-id provenance from §3.1.

3.3 Cross-region data residency across 200+ regions

SD-RTN routes through 200+ countries and regions. For each audit cycle, per-channel-region selection must be auditable per-channel-id against Schrems II + GDPR Art. 28 (processor obligations) + EU AI Act Art. 10 (data governance for high-risk AI systems) + India DPDP Act 2023 + Brazil LGPD Art. 33 + UAE PDPL + China PIPL + Singapore PDPA + Philippines DPA. Customers in regulated verticals (EU + India + Brazil + UAE + China + Singapore + Philippines) want per-tenant region-locking enforceable at the per-app-id + per-channel-id granularity, with per-cross-border-transfer-sccs-version-US-EU + per-cross-border-transfer-sccs-version-CN-PIPL tracked per transfer event.

3.4 HIPAA-eligible Agora Telehealth + Conversational AI SDK voice-agent-in-telehealth pipeline

The Agora Telehealth configuration must ship per-room-id PHI-flag + per-cloud-recording-id CMK/BYOK encryption + per-on-premise-recording-PHI-tag + BAA-ready configuration satisfying HIPAA §164.312(a)(2)(iv) (encryption in transit) + §164.312(b) (audit controls) + §164.312(e)(1) (transmission security) + §164.514(b) (minimum necessary). For voice-agent-in-telehealth pipelines (Conversational AI SDK + Telehealth), per-LLM-call-message-id must carry a PHI-flag downstream to per-tool-call-id + per-cloud-recording-id, with per-CRM-write-PHI-redaction-hook-id enforced at every downstream write. No other Tier-1 SFU vendor ships all four controls at HIPAA-grade out of the box.

3.5 Cross-tenant isolation across Agora Cloud + Enterprise + On-Premise

For Agora Cloud + Agora Enterprise + Agora On-Premise + per-app-id + per-certificate-id + per-token-id surfaces, audit committees want per-tenant isolation evidence per SOC 2 CC6.1 (logical access controls) + GDPR Art. 28 + HIPAA + EU AI Act Art. 10 + FedRAMP Moderate readiness, with breach-detection-event-id fed into a downstream SIEM (Splunk / Datadog / S3) for SOC 2 CC7.3 + CC7.4 incident-response evidence. The per-tenant-KMS-key-id + per-tenant-isolation-flag + per-account-tenant-isolation triple is the canonical evidence pattern.
