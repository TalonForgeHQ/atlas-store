Why Statuspage closes the incident-response cohort

Statuspage (an Atlassian product since 2016) sits in a different category than incident.io, Rootly, Better Stack, and FireHydrant — those four are incident-management platforms with status pages as a feature. Statuspage is the status page, and Atlassian acquired it precisely so every Opsgenie / PagerDuty buyer buying the rest of the Atlassian stack now has a native, Atlassian-Cloud-inheritance-boundary status-page product. The wedgable thing for a buyer evidence audit is: the trust pack lives at the Atlassian Cloud layer, not at the Statuspage product layer, and your audit needs to trace the inheritance explicitly. Most teams assume "Atlassian is in scope so Statuspage is in scope" — and then fail to document the inheritance boundary for their own audit committee.

Five buyer-facing evidence joins

  1. Inheritance boundary, not inherited trust. Map the Atlassian Cloud SOC 2 / ISO / FedRAMP attestation surface onto the Statuspage product surface. The boundary is in two places: (a) statuspage.io runs on Atlassian-managed AWS accounts that inherit the FedRAMP Moderate authorization boundary, and (b) Atlassian Cloud Audit Logs are the system-of-record only for Atlassian-Identity-layer actions, not Statuspage-tier subscriber events.
  2. Rovo + Atlassian AI EU AI Act Art. 14 evidence. Per-incident per-tenant human-oversight operational record: who accepted / edited / rejected / published the Rovo-drafted incident update, in what time-window, with what audit-trail fingerprint. Sourced from Atlassian Cloud Audit Logs + Statuspage REST API post endpoint + Rovo agent execution log if the customer has Atlassian Guard Premium.
  3. Art. 50 transparency-labeling for Statuspage Rovo outputs. Every Rovo-drafted post-mortem or subscriber email must be labelable as AI-generated. Audit check: does the published Statuspage post-mortem carry the Atlassian AI label visible to subscribers? Does the subscriber email show the Atlassian AI signature line?
  4. Per-subscriber status-change audit log. Statuspage has an Audit Log Streaming SKU feature (separate from Atlassian Cloud Audit Logs) that exposes subscription-level events: who subscribed, who unsubscribed, who hit a 4xx SMS gateway, who got the email for a particular incident. The SKU feature is the buyer-facing audit surface — the Atlassian Cloud Audit Logs are not.
  5. ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AIMS scope. Atlassian is named as ISO/IEC 42001:2023 certified on the Atlassian Cloud Trust Center (verified live 2026-07-21). For Statuspage, the AIMS scope attestation must declare: which Statuspage features use Atlassian AI + Rovo, what the human-oversight boundary is, and what the deletion-cascade latency is on incident records.
