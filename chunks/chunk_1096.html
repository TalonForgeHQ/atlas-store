Five-wedge non-overlap rubric for Rootly

  1. Slack-native incident workflow as the cohort-unique primary control surface. Rootly's primary key in the evidence row is the Slack channel + Slack workflow + Slack workflow run that fired, not the Kubernetes alert or the on-call schedule. This is a cohort-unique primitive: the row proves which Slack-native workflow drove the incident, which is the lane Resolve AI and incident.io do not ship.
  2. AI postmortem as a structured, auditable artifact. Rootly's "AI Postmortem" feature produces a structured postmortem with ai_postmortem_id + ai_summary_id + model_version + token_spend_usd join keys, joining the incident to the AI's narrative output for SOC 2 CC7.4 (incident recovery) auditability. This is the cohort-unique wedge.
  3. On-call + retros + AI summary + Slack workflow as a single Slack-native chain. Rootly combines the on-call schedule + retros + AI summary + Slack workflow into one Slack-native chain, distinct from incident.io's status-page-centric chain and Resolve AI's Kubernetes-satellite-centric chain.
  4. Native PagerDuty + Opsgenie migration tooling. Rootly ships explicit PagerDuty + Opsgenie migrators, joining the cohort-unique "anti-PagerDuty" wedge for teams mid-migration.
  5. Compliance posture for regulated ops: SOC 2 Type II + GDPR, with per-incident audit export and Slack-actor reconciliation across the evidence row.

Rootly versus the alternative choices

Choose Rootly when the missing evidence is the join from Slack workflow + AI postmortem + Slack actor to incident + remediation + human override, under SOC 2 Type II / GDPR controls, and where the buyer is mid-migration from PagerDuty or Opsgenie.

Choose Resolve AI when the missing evidence is the join from alert to investigate agent to RCA to remediation action to human override against Kubernetes, and the buyer cares more about the agent's actions than the Slack workflow that paged the human.

Choose incident.io when the missing evidence is the join from on-call schedule + escalation policy to page to AI summary to postmortem to status page to customer comm, and the buyer has just decided to leave PagerDuty or Opsgenie.

Choose traditional on-call + status page stacks (PagerDuty + Statuspage + Atlassian Status Page) when the requirement is page + status page only, with no AI summary and no postmortem-as-structured-artifact.

Choose observability platforms (Datadog, Honeycomb, Grafana, New Relic) when the requirement is dashboards, tracing, and metrics, not an incident-driven AI agent or a structured postmortem.

Choose eval/observability platforms for agents (Braintrust, Langfuse, Confident AI, Arize) when the work is development-time scoring of an LLM, not production incident response.
