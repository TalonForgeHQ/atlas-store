Seven questions to ask before approving an AI product-research vendor

  1. Can every replay be joined to consent and capture policy? Require participant_id, consent_event_id, session_replay_id, capture_policy_version_id, and tenant_id in one exportable row.
  2. Is PII redacted before storage and before model access? Distinguish client-side masking, ingestion-time redaction, storage-time controls, and prompt-time filtering. Record the redaction rule version and exception decision for every frame or event sent to an AI service.
  3. Can an AI summary cite its source? Join each ai_summary_id and ai_product_insight_id to the exact survey response, replay timestamp, transcript segment, model version, prompt version, and cited evidence.
  4. What prevents prompt injection through user feedback? Treat free-text surveys, DOM text, and replayed inputs as untrusted content. Require input delimiting, tool restrictions, output validation, and a human-approval gate under OWASP LLM01 and LLM06.
  5. Are tenants isolated through the full AI path? Check storage, vector indexes, caches, model gateways, evaluation datasets, and support tooling—not only the primary database—against SOC 2 CC6.1 and GDPR Art. 28.
  6. Can the vendor honor deletion and retention requests? A participant deletion must propagate to raw replay, extracted text, survey response, embedding, AI summary, evaluation copy, and backup-expiry record. Preserve an auditable tombstone without retaining the deleted personal data.
  7. Who approved the recommendation? Link product-roadmap exports, Jira or Linear writes, experiment changes, and customer-facing actions to human_approval_id, purpose, timestamp, and rollback record.

This single join supports SOC 2 CC7.2 event reconstruction, EU AI Act Art. 12 record keeping and Art. 14 human oversight, GDPR Arts. 5, 17, and 28, ISO 42001 performance evaluation, and enterprise vendor-due-diligence evidence requests.

Five failure modes buyers should test

  • A password, health detail, payment value, or private message appears unmasked in a replay frame.
  • A survey response contains adversarial instructions that alter the AI summary or suppress competing evidence.
  • An AI insight cites a cohort trend but cannot identify the underlying responses or replay timestamps.
  • A deleted participant remains present in embeddings, evaluation datasets, cached summaries, or exports.
  • A product recommendation reaches a downstream workflow without a named reviewer, approval timestamp, or rollback path.
