Five buyer-facing joins

  1. Identity → role → verified source → answer: user, role, connected source (Drive, Confluence, Salesforce, Zendesk, etc.), source ACL snapshot, Guru card, verification state, retrieval event, Knowledge Agent version, answer, citation, and tenant.
  2. Verification state and freshness: verifier identity, verification timestamp, source revision, freshness policy, unverify event, stale-content suppression rule, and the answer, chat, research artifact, or automation affected.
  3. Knowledge Agent reasoning and action authorization: agent instructions, model/provider version, retrieved evidence, tool or connector scope (browser, Slack, Teams, Salesforce, Jira, custom API), policy result, downstream object, and denial/retry reason.
  4. Human oversight and rollback: reviewer, approval, rejection, correction, escalation, compensating action, final state, and rollback target for Knowledge Agent-initiated work.
  5. Export and deletion: export and deletion across connected-source copies, Guru cards, embeddings, search caches, answers, chats, research artifacts, action traces, evaluations, logs, replicas, and backups.

Common evidence failures

  • Showing a Guru Knowledge Agent answer without the Guru card, the verification state at answer time, the freshness window, the connected source ACL snapshot, and the role/ACL applied to the requester.
  • Logging a Slack or Teams delivery without the channel scope, the channel-scoped role, the Guru card cited, and the verification timestamp attached to that card.
  • Recording a Knowledge Agent automation without the connector scope, the policy decision, the downstream object, and the rollback target.
  • Promoting a Knowledge Agent revision without the evaluation dataset, the reviewer, the verifier-of-record change list, and the rollback target.
  • Deleting a Guru card while omitting connected-source copies, embeddings, search caches, answers, chats, research artifacts, action traces, evaluations, replicas, or backups.
