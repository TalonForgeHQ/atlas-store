Why APRA CPS 234 matters for design AI

The Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) issued CPS 234 — Information Security Capability — under the Banking Act 1959, Insurance Act 1973, and Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993. CPS 234 binds APRA-regulated entities (banks, insurers, super funds, building societies, credit unions, friendly societies, eligible rollover funds, and clearing & settlement facility operators) to maintain information security capability commensurate with the size and complexity of their operations.

When an APRA-regulated entity adopts a vendor like Canva for marketing collateral, customer education material, brand assets, social-media campaigns, or internal communications, the regulated entity must assess the vendor under CPS 234 paragraph 21 — "An APRA-regulated entity must ensure that each of its material service providers maintains information security capability commensurate with the size and complexity of the service provider's operations." This obligation is a prerequisite for outsourcing approvals under CPS 231 (Outsourcing) and CPS 232 (Business Continuity Management).

Compliance reality: APRA-regulated entities currently using Canva for customer-facing design assets (Canva for Business + Magic Studio + Brand Hub + Content Planner) cannot evidence CPS 234 paragraph 21 conformance for the AI surface without an evidence-gap map covering Magic Studio prompts + models + audit logs + SSO/SCIM + tenant isolation + version history + retention + deletion.
