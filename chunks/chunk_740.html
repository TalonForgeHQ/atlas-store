The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — Source Text and Context to Suggestion to Model/Rule Version

For every Textio suggestion in a job description, candidate outreach, performance review, or employee communication, can the buyer export the source text and context, the Textio model or rule version, the suggestion text, the confidence, the language-signal classification, and the accepted or rejected edit decision?

Without this chain, an enterprise cannot prove which language version produced which suggestion, and the procurement question of "which Textio model or rule version touched this candidate-facing document" remains unanswered.

Gap 2 — Candidate/Employee Segment to Language Signal to Fairness/Inclusion Check to Reviewer Decision

When Textio flags a phrase as biased, exclusionary, or sub-optimal for a candidate or employee segment, can the buyer replay the segment definition, the language signal category, the fairness or inclusion check version, the demographic or role-cohort filter applied, the reviewer decision (accepted, rejected, edited), and the resulting artifact?

This is the human-oversight record for EEOC + OFCCP + Title VII compliance, GDPR Art. 22 automated-decision-making evidence, NYC Local Law 144 + Illinois AI Video Interview Act bias-audit evidence, and EU AI Act Art. 14(4) human-oversight requirements. The first-party Textio inclusive-recruiting claim is the operational evidence that fairness review exists; the procurement question is whether it is exported at the per-suggestion, per-segment, per-reviewer level.

Gap 3 — Job or Performance Document to Collaborator Identity to Approval to Published Version

For every job description, performance review template, candidate outreach sequence, or employee communication where multiple collaborators edited the document, can the buyer export the collaborator identity, role, tenant, region, edit timestamp, approval status, and the published version hash?

The evidence should distinguish an original draft from a Textio-suggested edit from a human override, and should expose which collaborator approved the final published version. This is the SOC 2 change-management evidence, ISO 27001 access-control evidence, and 21st Century Workplace Fairness Act (NY) recordkeeping requirement.

Gap 4 — Tenant/Role/Region to Text-Processing Policy to Retention/Deletion Receipt

When Textio processes text from a candidate application, an employee review, or a performance document, can the buyer prove the tenant, role, region, text-processing policy version, retention window, deletion request, deletion completion event, and downstream propagation receipt (transcripts, audit logs, backups, embeddings, model training data)?

This is the GDPR Art. 17 right-to-erasure evidence, CCPA/CPRA right-to-delete evidence, PIPEDA + Australia Privacy Act + Singapore PDPA + LGPD + China PIPL retention evidence, and the EU AI Act Art. 12 + Annex IV data-governance requirement. The procurement question is whether Textio's deletion cascade reaches every system that touched the candidate or employee text.

Gap 5 — Model or Policy Change to Affected Workflows to Rollback/Remediation/Customer Notice

When Textio ships a model or rule update, a fairness-check policy change, or a language-signal reclassification, can the buyer export the affected workflows, the affected candidate or employee documents, the rollback event, the remediation action, the customer notice timestamp, and the customer-acknowledged receipt?
