Gap 1 — Rated entity and asset-observation provenance

For each rated organization, preserve the matched legal entity, company identifier, domains and attributed assets, observation source, observation timestamp, confidence, scoring-model version, and reviewer. The record should distinguish customer-supplied inventory from independently observed infrastructure.

  • Rated-entity ID, legal name, domain, asset, and relationship owner
  • Observation source, timestamp, confidence, and attribution rule
  • Scoring-model and data-pipeline version
  • Tenant-scoped registry snapshot and export checksum

Gap 2 — Rating change to remediation evidence

For every material rating change, retain the prior and new rating, contributing findings, affected assets, observation window, policy threshold, assigned owner, remediation evidence, re-observation result, and human disposition. A historical chart alone does not explain why a decision changed.

  • Prior rating, new rating, delta, and materiality threshold
  • Finding IDs, affected assets, and observation timestamps
  • Remediation owner, submitted evidence, and re-scan result
  • Human approval, exception, or residual-risk acceptance receipt

Gap 3 — Forward-looking TPRM decision lineage

When a dashboard or predictive signal informs onboarding, renewal, escalation, or termination, capture the rated entity, decision date, source indicators, model or policy version, score and confidence, decision rationale, reviewer, exception status, and downstream contract or ticket reference.

  • Decision type: onboard, renew, monitor, escalate, or terminate
  • Source indicators, policy version, score, and confidence
  • Reviewer identity, rationale, and exception status
  • Downstream procurement, contract, or remediation object ID

Gap 4 — MCP tool call to accountable agent action

For each BitSight MCP tool call, retain the tool name and version, requesting agent and tenant, query input hash, returned rating or finding IDs, authorization scope, region, policy result, human approval where required, and resulting external state change. This separates intelligence retrieval from a binding risk decision.

  • MCP request ID, tool/version, agent/run ID, and tenant ID
  • Query hash, returned evidence IDs, and response timestamp
  • Credential scope, region, policy/guardrail result, and reviewer
  • Downstream ticket, vendor status, or contract-state receipt

Gap 5 — Board, underwriter, and examiner export

A defensible packet should include a dated vendor-registry snapshot, rating and model versions, material findings, decisions, exceptions, remediation status, human approvals, source timestamps, and an export checksum. The same source snapshot should regenerate the same packet without screenshots or manual reconstruction.

  • Dated vendor and rated-entity inventory snapshot
  • Material rating changes and decision receipts
  • Exceptions, remediation state, approvals, and source freshness
  • Export version, generated-at timestamp, and checksum

Five-question procurement test

  1. Can one rated entity be reproduced from legal-entity match through attributed asset and source observation?
  2. Can one material rating change be traced to findings, scoring version, remediation, and human disposition?
  3. Can one forward-looking decision be traced to its policy, evidence, reviewer, rationale, and downstream object?
  4. Can one MCP call be traced from authorized agent query through returned evidence to any resulting state change?
  5. Can the board or examiner packet be regenerated from versioned records with the same checksum?
