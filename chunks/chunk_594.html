2. Prompt-injection defense

Untrusted input channels for Visme AI Designer + AI Writer + Brand Wizard surface include: untrusted user text in Visme AI Designer seed text, untrusted Visme AI Writer inputs, untrusted Visme AI Resize inputs, untrusted Visme AI Edit inputs, untrusted Visme AI Translate inputs, untrusted Visme AI Image Generator inputs, untrusted Visme Brand Wizard inputs, and untrusted connected-app payloads from Google Workspace/Microsoft 365/Slack/Teams/Zoom/HubSpot/Mailchimp/YouTube/Vimeo/Wistia/Loom integrations. EU AI Act Art. 14 requires documented prompt-injection mitigation.

3. Workspace/team/brand/Enterprise-tenant isolation

Visme serves individual users, teams, and Enterprise tenants (Brand Kit + SSO + SAML + audit logs). SOC 2 CC7.2 requires documented tenant-isolation controls. EU AI Act Art. 14 requires human-oversight per tenant.

4. Deletion/retention/rollback/version-history/asset-rollback/AI-generation-retention

When a user deletes a Visme design, the AI generation history must also be deleted or anonymized per GDPR Art. 17 + CCPA/CPRA. When a user rolls back a Visme AI Designer output, the AI-generation must be removed from the audit log. EU AI Act Art. 12 requires retention policy documentation.

5. Immutable human-approval evidence

For each Visme AI Designer output, AI Writer draft, AI Image generation, AI Resize variant, AI Edit fill, AI Translate output — there must be an immutable human-approval record before publication to the connected channel (Google Workspace/Microsoft 365/Slack/Teams/Zoom/HubSpot/Mailchimp/YouTube/Vimeo/Wistia/Loom).

6. Per-agent/per-model/per-tool-call/per-design/per-team/per-workspace/per-Brand-Kit/per-Enterprise-tenant cost attribution
