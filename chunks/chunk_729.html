The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — Bedside Voice/Text Instruction to Flowsheet Row

Can Aiva pin the bedside voice or text instruction, the patient resolution, the encounter resolution, the Epic-or-Cerner flowsheet + row, the discrete value or narrative note, the confidence, the redaction event, and the retention decision for every nursing charting event?

Without this chain, a hospital cannot prove which spoken or typed instruction supported a flowsheet mutation, and the first-party "81% faster documentation" and "37% higher Press Ganey" claims cannot be tied to a specific row.

Gap 2 — Aiva Assistant Configuration + LLM Sub-Processor Lineage

For every discrete value, narrative note, patient-education step, and clinical-reference lookup, can the buyer replay the Aiva Assistant configuration version, the LLM sub-processor version (Aiva's choice of OpenAI / Anthropic / in-house / open-weight), the prompt or extraction policy version, the source span, the confidence, the citation, and the note-draft version?

The evidence should distinguish an original spoken instruction from an inferred or normalized clinical concept, and should expose which LLM was used for which section of the charting event.

Gap 3 — Nurse Review and Human Oversight

Can Aiva export who, when, role, tenant, region, and identity assurance approved, edited, rejected, or added to a generated flowsheet row or narrative note, with a pre-review and post-review artifact pair?

This is the human-oversight record for HIPAA accountability, SOC 2 change evidence, ISO/IEC 42001 governance, and EU AI Act Article 14(4). The first-party "67% of nurses saying they enjoy the product" claim is the operational evidence that nurse-oversight exists; the procurement question is whether it is exported at the per-row, per-note level.

Gap 4 — Epic/Cerner Write-Back and Patient-Safety Receipt

Can a hospital tie the final nurse-approved charting event to the exact Epic or Cerner integration, the encounter ID, the destination flowsheet + row, the write-back result, the rollback event, the duplicate-prevention rule, and the safety-exception receipt?

Nursing documentation evidence is incomplete if the downstream flowsheet mutation cannot be reconstructed. Epic and Cerner are the named EHR partners; the procurement question is what receipt Aiva exports per write-back, including the published "MEDITECH coming soon" integration timeline evidence.

Gap 5 — Bedside Smart-Room + ServiceNow Action and Deletion Cascade

When a nurse triggers a smart-room action, a ServiceNow request, a Zebra device workflow, or a patient-education step, can Aiva prove the device or workflow target, the execution state, the human escalation, and the deletion propagation across transcript, telemetry, audit logs, backups, and downstream EHR artifacts?

The receipt should include the request, the policy version, the region, the LLM sub-processor, the deletion event, the completion timestamp, and any legally retained audit record.
