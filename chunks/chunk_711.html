The Five Audit Gaps

Gap 1 — Control-to-Evidence-to-Auditor Provenance

For every SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, HITRUST, PCI DSS, CMMC, NIST CSF 2.0, or GDPR assertion, a buyer should be able to retrieve the framework version, control version, source integration, evidence checksum, collection timestamp, control owner, reviewer, auditor request, auditor decision, and final-report reference. A dashboard state is not enough; the export needs the complete chain.

Gap 2 — Audit Lifecycle Platform State-Change Receipts

Every ALP state transition should preserve actor, role, prior state, new state, supporting evidence, exception rationale, remediation owner, due date, approval, and immutable timestamp. This becomes especially important when the customer and auditor collaborate from day one: edits made after auditor access must remain attributable rather than silently replacing the earlier record.

Gap 3 — Pentest Finding to Compliance Closure

A penetration-test finding should join scanner or tool version, scope, affected asset, severity method, exploit evidence, tester identity, retest result, remediation ticket or commit, compensating control, auditor acceptance, and final attestation. Otherwise the pentest PDF and compliance workspace become two separate evidence islands.

Gap 4 — Independence and Role Separation

When one platform spans readiness support and expert audit delivery, enterprise buyers will ask for clear role receipts. The evidence pack should distinguish customer control owner, readiness advisor, penetration tester, auditor, reviewer, approver, and report issuer, including conflict declarations, recusals, escalations, and overrides.

Gap 5 — Multi-Framework Inheritance and Change Impact

When one artifact supports several frameworks, Thoropass should export each mapping-rule version, inheritance rationale, jurisdictional exception, stale-evidence event, retest trigger, and downstream report impact. A framework update should identify which controls, evidence objects, tests, exceptions, and reports became stale — not merely change the dashboard's completion percentage.
