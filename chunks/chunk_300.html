Audit gap #1 — Per-Participant-ID provenance join-table

UserTesting's Human Insight Platform + Live Conversation AI-moderation pipeline touches every session, every moderated-recording, and every AI-synthesized-insight-card. To pass EU AI Act Art. 12 (log-keeping obligations for high-risk AI systems) + ISO 42001 9.4 (event-traceability) + SOC 2 CC7.2 (system-operations evidence), the canonical pattern is a 12-column join-table that captures, per participant-session:

  • participant_consent_link_id — the canonical consent-link captured at recruitment (Respondent.io pattern) or at session-start (UserZoom pattern)
  • conversation_id — the Live Conversation session identifier
  • ai_moderation_decision_id — every AI-moderation-pipeline decision (relevance scoring, sentiment, theme classification) with timestamp + model-version + confidence
  • synthesis_output_id — every AI-synthesized-insight-card (UserTesting AI Insights / Highlight Reels) with model + prompt-version + cost
  • human_oversight_approval_id — every human-researcher-review-action (the human-in-the-loop pattern required by EU AI Act Art. 14)
  • customer_tenant_id — the customer-account that owns the workspace the participant contributed to
  • procurement_vendor_dd_event_id — every customer-tenant-procurement-vendor-DD-question that referenced this participant-session (so audit-question → evidence-traceability is one-click)
  • audit_log_entry_id — the WORM (write-once-read-many) audit-log entry for the participant-session
  • residency_region_id — the data-residency-region the session was processed in (US-AWS / EU-AWS / AP-AWS)
  • retention_decision_id — when the participant-session was deleted, who triggered it, and the cross-system confirmation (SOC 2 CC7.3 + GDPR Art. 17 right-to-erasure)
  • ai_disclosure_event_id — when the participant was informed they were talking to an AI-moderator (CA SB 1001 + CO SB 24-205 + IL AI Video Interview Act + TX + NY + 7 other states; EU AI Act Art. 50 transparency)
  • cross_border_transfer_id — every Schrems-II-relevant cross-border data transfer (US-EU, EU-US, EU-AP) with SCC reference + TIA reference

The Atlas audit ships a working 12-column table seeded with the UserTesting-platform's event-stream + AI-moderation-decision-log, with one row per participant-session-evidence-event, queryable for any procurement-vendor-DD question in under 5 minutes.

Audit gap #2 — AI-moderation training-corpus + fine-tune-license-provenance

Under EU AI Act Art. 53(d) GPAI training-data transparency + Art. 53(1)(b) copyright-summary, every GPAI provider must publish a "sufficiently detailed summary" of training data sufficient for copyright-holders to exercise their Art. 53(1)(c) opt-out. With Thoma Bravo's portfolio consolidation of UserTesting + UserZoom + Askable + Respondia, the cross-platform AI-moderation training-corpus spans ~5M moderated-conversations and ~50M participant-recordings (estimated based on UserTesting's published ~3M participants + UserZoom's ~1.5M + Askable's ~500K + Respondia's enterprise panel).

The Atlas audit asks three concrete questions:

  1. Source corpora: What fraction of the AI-moderator training data is (a) UserTesting-UserZoom-Askable-Respondia moderated-conversations, (b) licensed third-party datasets (e.g. academic-UX-research corpuses), (c) synthetic data from GPT-4-class models, (d) open-source-licensed datasets (e.g. Anthropic Claude Conversations)?
  2. Licensing chain: For each source, what's the licensing chain back to the participant-original-consent? Specifically, does the original-consent-link-URL cover (a) the moderation-decision being used for training, (b) the synthesis-output being used for training, (c) the cross-platform-portfolio-sharing?
  3. Opt-out mechanism: Per Art. 53(1)(c), how does a participant exercise the right to opt their data out of training? Is the mechanism per-session, per-account, or per-cohort? How long does the opt-out take to propagate across all four portfolio entities?

Deliverable: a 4-page training-data-summary.html published at usertesting.com/ai-act/training-data-summary with the source breakdown + licensing-chain + opt-out-mechanism evidence, suitable for direct procurement-vendor-DD response.

Audit gap #3 — Cross-tenant UserTesting SaaS + Thoma-Bravo-portfolio tenant-isolation

Post-Thoma Bravo acquisition (2022, ~$1.1B), UserTesting shares infrastructure + AI-moderation-pipelines + a single ML-platform-team with UserZoom + Askable + Respondia. Under SOC 2 CC6.1 (logical-and-physical-access-controls) + GDPR Art. 28 (processor obligations) + Schrems II (US-EU data-transfer), the cross-entity tenant-isolation is the question every EU enterprise-procurement-vendor-DD pipeline is asking right now.

The Atlas audit walks four isolation dimensions:

  • AWS-account isolation: Are UserTesting / UserZoom / Askable / Respondia on separate AWS accounts, or shared with logical isolation via IAM? (Separate accounts is the SOC 2 CC6.1 gold-standard; logical isolation is acceptable with strong ABAC evidence.)
  • KMS-key isolation: Are encryption-at-rest KMS keys per-entity or per-customer-tenant? (Per-customer-tenant is the gold-standard; per-entity is acceptable.)
  • AI-moderation-inference-endpoint isolation: Does the AI-moderator run on shared inference-endpoints (with logical tenant-routing) or per-entity inference-endpoints? (Shared is acceptable with strict tenant-routing + per-tenant usage-meters; per-entity is better.)
  • AI-training-pipeline isolation: Are the four entities' training-pipelines logically-isolated or shared? (Critical because if training-data-pooling happens, GDPR-Art. 28 processor obligations change.)

Deliverable: a 6-page tenant-isolation-evidence.html with the four-dimension table + per-dimension SOC 2 CC6.1 control-reference + per-dimension Schrems-II cross-border-transfer evidence.

Audit gap #4 — AI-disclosure + participant-consent under 12-state + EU AI Act Art. 50

UserTesting's Live Conversation feature deploys an AI-moderator in real-time video sessions with participants across 50 US states + 40 countries. The 12-state AI-disclosure patchwork (CA SB 1001 + CO SB 24-205 + IL AI Video Interview Act + TX + NY + 7 others) + EU AI Act Art. 50 transparency-obligation require that participants be informed they're talking to an AI-moderator before the conversation begins.

The Atlas audit walks the disclosure-flow:

  1. Pre-conversation disclosure: Is the AI-disclosure shown before the participant clicks "Start Session"? Is the disclosure text the canonical-12-state-compliant text (e.g. "You are about to have a video session moderated by an AI. A human will review all AI-moderation decisions.")?
  2. In-conversation disclosure: Does the AI-moderator verbally identify itself as AI within the first 60 seconds of the session? (CA SB 1001 requires this for AI-video-interview contexts.)
  3. Post-conversation disclosure: Does the participant receive a written record that an AI-moderator was present? Is the record retained per WORM retention?
  4. Cross-platform consistency: Do UserZoom + Askable + Respondia apply the same disclosure-flow, or does each entity implement independently?

Deliverable: a 3-page ai-disclosure-evidence.html with the disclosure-flow for each portfolio entity + per-state compliance-matrix + per-EU-member-state compliance-matrix.

Audit gap #5 — WORM retention + cost-attribution join-table

UserTesting processes ~3M participant-sessions/year × ~10 AI-moderation-decisions/session = ~30M AI-moderation-decisions/year. Under SOC 2 CC7.2 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + SEC 17a-4 WORM retention + EU AI Act Annex III 4 (high-risk-system log-retention), every AI-moderation-decision must be retained for 6-10 years in WORM storage with per-decision cost-attribution for chargeback / cost-of-goods-sold accounting.

The Atlas audit ships a 6-column WORM cost-attribution join-table:

  • ai_moderation_decision_id — the AI-moderator-decision identifier
  • model_version — the AI-moderator model-version (e.g. usertesting-moderator-gpt-j-2024-11)
  • compute_cost_usd — the per-decision-compute-cost in USD (per AWS Cost-Explorer allocation-tag)
  • customer_tenant_id — the customer-account-chargeback-target
  • worm_retention_until — the WORM-retention-expiry-date (6 years for SOC 2 / 10 years for EU AI Act Art. 12 high-risk / 7 years for SEC 17a-4 if applicable)
  • audit_query_count_90d — the 90-day rolling count of audit-queries-against-this-decision (so high-query-cost-decisions surface to storage-tiering decisions)

Deliverable: a working WORM cost-attribution-table seeded with the UserTesting-platform's AI-moderation-decision-stream + AWS-Cost-Explorer-allocation-tags + per-decision-chargeback-report.
