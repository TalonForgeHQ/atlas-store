Why this spec exists (and why no workflow-automation vendor ships it fully yet)

Every workflow-automation vendor in the market — n8n Cloud, n8n Self-Hosted, n8n Enterprise (all founded by Jan Oberhauser, Founder & CEO since 2019, Berlin Germany, bootstrapped to seed in 2021 then Series A from Sequoia €15M in 2022 then Series B from Accel + HV Capital €55M in 2024 reaching $750M-$1B valuation per Bloomberg), plus the broader cohort of Bardeen, Zapier, Workato, Tray.io, Make.com, Pipedream, Browse.ai — collects per-execution telemetry. Each stores it slightly differently. Each gives you a different slice of the join-table needed to reconstruct what actually happened when an AI-agent workflow executed in production against a regulated-industry customer.

The problem shows up the first time a regulated-industry customer (a bank, a hospital, an insurance carrier, a federal agency, an EU AI Act high-risk deployer) runs into one of these audit questions:

  • "A customer filed a complaint about an AI-agent-generated claim denial on March 4. Show me the exact per-workflow-id + per-node-id + per-execution-id + per-trigger-id chain that produced the decision, and prove the LLM call (per-LLM-call-id) routed through the per-prompt-template-version-commit-id that was active when the call was made, not v6."
  • "Our EU regulator wants proof that the AI-agent workflow we A/B tested against a high-risk customer cohort did not produce biased outputs. Reconstruct every per-execution-id in cohort B and join it to per-LLM-call-id + per-prompt-id + per-token-usage-id + per-tool-call-id + per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id."
  • "An SOC 2 CC7.2 auditor is asking us to demonstrate that an AI-agent workflow with destructive tool calls (delete-record, send-email, post-public-message, transfer-funds) required a human-in-the-loop checkpoint. Show me per-tool-call-id + per-human-in-the-loop-approval-id + per-approval-timestamp-utc + per-approver-user-id for every destructive action."
  • "A SOC 2 CC7.2 + EU AI Act Art. 12 auditor wants to confirm our credential-vault isolation didn't leak cross-tenant. Prove that every per-credential-id is joined to per-workspace-id + per-user-id + per-team-id + per-workflow-execution-id so we can demonstrate per-tenant isolation-evidence."
  • "We had a hallucination incident in our patient-facing AI-agent workflow. Reconstruct every per-LLM-call-id that went into the response, including the per-tool-call-id + per-tool-call-result-id + per-MCP-server-call-id chain and the per-eval-result-id + per-eval-run-id that should have caught it."

None of the seven workflow-automation vendors above ships all 60+ columns of this join-table out of the box. Most ship 15-25 of them. Some don't even expose per-prompt-template-version-id and per-prompt-template-commit-id as separate fields — they conflate them. Others don't expose per-credential-id at all, so credential-rotation provenance is unrecoverable. n8n leads on LangChain integration + per-node custom-code extensibility + fair-code self-hosting, but even n8n's AI Agent Node telemetry needs the explicit join-table below reconstructed by the operator for full SOC 2 + EU AI Act + ISO 42001 audit readiness.

The 60+ column join-table (the actual spec for n8n)

The table below is the minimum viable schema for "production AI-agent-workflow audit-target reconstruction" on n8n Cloud + n8n Self-Hosted + n8n Enterprise that survives vendor-DD + SOC 2 + GDPR Art. 30 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + ISO 42001 + OWASP LLM Top 10. It is organized into 8 logical groups:

Group 1: Per-workflow execution identity (10 columns)

per-workflow-id (UUID, primary key) | per-workspace-id | per-user-id (workflow owner) | per-team-id | per-environment-id (dev/staging/prod) | per-deployment-id | per-region-id | per-execution-id (UUID per run) | per-execution-timestamp-utc | per-execution-latency-ms

Group 2: Per-node execution provenance (8 columns)

per-node-id (per-AI-Agent-Node + per-LangChain-Chain-Node + per-OpenAI-Node + per-Anthropic-Node + per-HTTP-Request-Node + per-Webhook-Node + per-Cron-Trigger-Node + per-Error-Trigger-Node) | per-node-type-id | per-node-position-in-workflow-id | per-node-input-schema-version-id | per-node-output-schema-version-id | per-node-error-id (if failed) | per-node-retry-count-id | per-node-custom-code-version-id (for JavaScript/Python custom nodes)

Group 3: Per-trigger provenance (8 columns)

per-trigger-id (Cron / Webhook / Email-Trigger / Manual / Schedule / Polling / Event-from-app / per-app-event-trigger) | per-trigger-source-id (origin app: Stripe / HubSpot / Salesforce / Notion / Slack / Linear / etc.) | per-trigger-payload-id (the inbound JSON) | per-trigger-auth-method-id (API-key / OAuth / HMAC / mTLS / JWT) | per-trigger-rate-limit-id | per-trigger-deduplication-id (same payload within window = no duplicate execution) | per-trigger-timestamp-utc | per-trigger-source-ip-id

Group 4: Per-LLM-call provenance (10 columns)

per-LLM-call-id (UUID) | per-LLM-call-model-provider-id (OpenAI / Anthropic / Bedrock / Vertex / Azure / Mistral / Groq / Together / Replicate / Fireworks / Cohere / Local Ollama / Hugging Face) | per-model-id (gpt-4o / claude-opus-4 / llama-3.1-70b / mistral-large-2 / etc.) | per-prompt-template-id | per-prompt-template-version-id | per-prompt-template-commit-id (git SHA) | per-prompt-environment-id (dev/staging/prod prompt registry) | per-prompt-A-B-test-id | per-token-usage-id (prompt-tokens + completion-tokens) | per-LLM-call-cost-usd

Group 5: Per-tool-call provenance (8 columns)

per-tool-call-id | per-tool-name-id (per-Anthropic-Claude-tool-use / per-OpenAI-Function-Calling-tool / per-LangChain-tool / per-MCP-server-tool / per-AI-Agent-Node-internal-tool) | per-tool-input-id | per-tool-result-id | per-tool-error-id (if failed) | per-tool-retry-count-id | per-tool-call-latency-ms | per-tool-destructive-flag-id (TRUE for delete-record, send-email, post-public, transfer-funds, requires human-in-the-loop)

Group 6: Per-credential + per-API-call provenance (6 columns)

per-credential-id (UUID stored in n8n credentials vault) | per-credential-type-id (OAuth2 / API-key / Basic-auth / mTLS / JWT) | per-credential-rotation-timestamp-utc | per-API-call-id | per-API-call-endpoint-id (the URL hit) | per-API-call-response-status-id (HTTP 200/4xx/5xx)

Group 7: Per-error + per-retry + per-billing provenance (5 columns)

per-error-id (UUID per-error) | per-error-stack-trace-id | per-error-classification-id (transient / permanent / rate-limit / auth-expired / per-LLM-call-failed / per-tool-call-failed) | per-retry-id | per-billing-event-id (per-execution cost + per-LLM-call-cost + per-tool-call-cost summed)

Group 8: Per-prompt-injection + per-RAG-retrieval + per-eval-result provenance (5 columns)

per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id (per-OWASP-LLM01-detector / per-Garak-scan-result / per-Lakera-Guard-result / per-Rebuff-result) | per-RAG-retrieval-event-id (per-vector-store-query-id + per-chunk-id-retrieved) | per-RAG-rerank-event-id | per-eval-result-id (per-LLM-as-judge / per-rule-based-eval / per-human-review) | per-eval-run-id (the eval suite identifier)

n8n-specific implementation notes (what you actually configure)

For n8n Cloud, the per-execution-id + per-node-id telemetry is exposed via the n8n REST API at /api/v1/executions/{execution_id} with a JSON payload containing per-node-status + per-node-start-time + per-node-end-time + per-node-input-bytes + per-node-output-bytes + per-node-error-message. For per-LLM-call-id + per-prompt-template-version-id, you need to wire the n8n AI Agent Node's metadata into a structured logger (Datadog / Splunk / Loki / CloudWatch / Sentry) at the per-node-output-execution hook, persisting per-prompt-id + per-token-usage-id + per-model-id + per-LLM-call-cost-usd as structured JSON. For per-tool-call-id + per-human-in-the-loop-approval-id, configure the n8n Wait Node + per-approval-webhook (Slack interactive message + per-approver-user-id + per-approval-timestamp-utc + per-approval-decision-id = approved | denied | timed-out) before any destructive tool call. For per-credential-id rotation provenance, n8n's credentials vault stores per-credential-id + per-credential-type-id + per-credential-created-at + per-credential-last-used-at but does NOT automatically log per-credential-rotation-timestamp-utc — you need a per-credential-update webhook to capture rotation events. For per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id, wire an upstream prompt-injection-detection step (Lakera Guard / Garak / Rebuff / Prompt Armor / Protect AI Layer) before any AI Agent Node execution and persist the per-detection-signal-id + per-detection-confidence-score to the structured log.

5 audit gaps that the spec surfaces (and what to do about them)

  1. Gap 1: Per-execution provenance join-table — n8n exposes per-execution-id + per-node-id but does NOT natively produce the 60-column join-table above. Fix: instrument every n8n AI Agent Node with a structured-logger emit-hook writing per-LLM-call-id + per-prompt-id + per-token-usage-id + per-tool-call-id + per-error-id + per-retry-id to a sidecar store (Postgres + S3 + Datadog Logs + Splunk + Loki) keyed by per-execution-id, then build a join view in your observability layer.
  2. Gap 2: AI-agent prompt-injection defense — n8n AI Agent Nodes flow untrusted content (webhook payloads, Notion pages, email bodies, scraped web content, third-party API responses) directly into LLM calls + tool calls. Fix: insert a prompt-injection-detection node (Lakera Guard / Garak / Rebuff) BEFORE every AI Agent Node execution; tag per-detection-signal-id + per-detection-confidence-score; enforce per-tool-destructive-flag-id requiring human-in-the-loop approval via n8n Wait Node for any tool with destructive-flag=TRUE.
  3. Gap 3: Cross-region workflow-execution data residency — n8n Cloud is currently EU-only on Azure West Europe (Frankfurt). Fix: for US / APAC customer cohort, deploy n8n Self-Hosted on Azure US-East or AWS ap-southeast-1 with per-region bucket selection enforced at the per-workflow-id level (a workflow tagged region=EU cannot execute on US-region infrastructure); document the per-region-bucket-selection evidence for Schrems II + GDPR Art. 28 + EU AI Act Art. 10 + India DPDP Act 2023.
  4. Gap 4: HIPAA eligibility + BAA — n8n Cloud + n8n Self-Hosted are not currently marketed as HIPAA-eligible. Fix: deploy n8n Self-Hosted on Azure BAA-backed infrastructure with per-credential-id CMK/BYOK encryption-at-rest, per-workflow-id PHI-flag enforcement (workflows tagged PHI=TRUE cannot log payload contents), per-execution-id audit-log retention at 6-year HIPAA §164.316(b)(2)(i) minimum, per-credential-id rotation cadence documented, and execute a BAA with n8n GmbH for the Cloud variant OR a BAA with Microsoft Azure for the Self-Hosted variant.
  5. Gap 5: Cross-tenant isolation evidence — n8n Cloud provides per-workspace-id isolation but the SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + EU AI Act Art. 10 evidence package for cross-tenant isolation needs to be requested from n8n GmbH (or assembled for n8n Self-Hosted via per-Kubernetes-namespace isolation + per-network-policy isolation + per-postgres-row-level-security-policy). Fix: for n8n Cloud, request the latest SOC 2 Type II report + EU AI Act Art. 10 + 12 + 15 technical-documentation evidence package from privacy@n8n.io; for n8n Self-Hosted, document the per-isolation-layer evidence yourself with periodic third-party pen-test + per-tenant-isolation-evidence review.
