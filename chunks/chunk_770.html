  1. Per-search + per-AI-summary provenance across the Fortune-100 corpus.   2. Per-tenant data isolation across Fortune-100 customer corpus. Bloomfire's positioning is "9x productivity gains" on enterprise knowledge corpus. The audit must verify: per-tenant embedding-store isolation, per-tenant retrieval-result re-ranking scoped to tenant data, per-tenant LLM-context window scoped to tenant data, per-tenant audit-log retention scoped to tenant's contractual window. The critical question is: when a Fortune-100 buyer's CISO asks for an export of all AI-generated content their employees consumed, is the export scoped to that tenant only or can it bleed into another tenant's training cache?
  3. Insights-engine + market-research content lineage. Bloomfire's CMO Dan Stradtman bio specifically positions the platform as an "insights engine" for market research. The audit must verify: source license terms per primary research vendor (Nielsen / IQVIA / Kantar / Mintel / etc.) + internal analyst decks, downstream redistribution rights per LLM-summary consumer, per-insight citation provenance, and redistribution embargo windows per market-research vendor contract. The Fortune-100 buyer's own content-redistribution policy when their AI-summary is forwarded to a downstream vendor or external partner must be auditable.
  4. EU AI Act Art. 14 + Art. 50 + Art. 53(1)(b) evidence cascade for the AI-augmented surface. Per EU AI Act Aug 2 2026 enforcement, Bloomfire's AI-augmented surface (AI search + AI summary + auto-tagging + auto-categorization) is in scope as a GPAI-deployment. The audit must verify: per-deployment human-oversight operational record (user accepts/rejects/edits AI output → provable human-in-the-loop log per session), Art. 50 transparency-labeling on every AI output ("AI-generated, review before use"), and Art. 53(1)(b) GPAI training-data transparency cascade across OpenAI / Anthropic / Google / self-hosted / Mistral / DeepSeek underlying models.
