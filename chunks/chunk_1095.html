Five-wedge non-overlap rubric for incident.io

  1. On-call schedule + escalation policy as the first-class control surface. incident.io's primary key in the evidence row is the on-call schedule + escalation policy that fired, not the alert itself. This is a cohort-unique primitive: the row proves which human (or rotation) was responsible at the moment the page went out.
  2. Status page + customer comm as a single replayable chain. incident.io's first-party pages describe integrated status pages and customer comms, and the cohort-unique wedge is the join from status_page_incident_id to customer_comm_send_id in one row — auditors ask for this and rarely receive it.
  3. Postmortem document as a structured artifact, not a wiki page. incident.io's postmortem template is a structured object with sections, action items, and a rca evidence chain, distinct from a free-text Confluence page that drifts out of date.
  4. AI summary as an auditable artifact, not a chat transcript. incident.io's AI summary carries an ai_summary_id and a token_spend_usd row, so a SOC 2 auditor can reconcile "what the AI said" against the underlying input and the model's version.
  5. Native migration path from PagerDuty + Opsgenie. incident.io ships an explicit PagerDuty Rescue Program + Opsgenie Rescue Program, which is a strong buyer's co-sign that the schema is interoperable with the legacy stack — and the cohort-unique wedge for teams who are mid-migration.

incident.io versus the alternative choices

Choose incident.io when the missing evidence is the join from on-call schedule + escalation policy to page to AI summary to postmortem to status page to customer comm, under SOC 2 Type II / ISO 27001 controls, and where the buyer has just decided to leave PagerDuty or Opsgenie.

Choose Resolve AI when the missing evidence is the join from alert to investigate agent to RCA to remediation action to human override against Kubernetes, and the buyer cares more about the agent's actions than the on-call schedule that paged the human.

Choose traditional on-call + status page stacks (PagerDuty + Statuspage + Atlassian Status Page) when the requirement is page + status page only, with no AI summary and no postmortem-as-structured-artifact.

Choose observability platforms (Datadog, Honeycomb, Grafana, New Relic) when the requirement is dashboards, tracing, and metrics, not an incident-driven AI agent or a structured postmortem.

Choose eval/observability platforms for agents (Braintrust, Langfuse, Confident AI, Arize) when the work is development-time scoring of an LLM, not production incident response.

The receipt is deliberately narrow enough to implement for one production incident while preserving the causal chain. It starts at the tenant and incident, follows the on-call schedule + escalation policy that fired the page, names the service and severity, follows the channel that carried the page, records the AI summary and RCA doc + evidence chain, then ends with the remediation action, human override, status page incident, postmortem id, customer impact, customer comm send, AI token spend, latency, and audit export.

Questions the replay should answer

  1. Which on-call schedule + escalation policy fired the page, and which channel carried it to which human?
  2. Which AI summary was produced, and which rca evidence chain was linked to the postmortem document?
  3. Which status page update and which customer comm went out, and can a SOC 2 auditor reconcile the row to the audit export?
  4. Which remediation action was proposed or executed, and which human override approved it?
  5. Is there cross-tenant bleed in the AI summary or the postmortem evidence chain?
