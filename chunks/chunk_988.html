Keeper AI-agent secrets audit: JIT credentials to recorded sessions

Keeper's first-party AI-agent page says it secures AI agents with zero-trust access, ephemeral credentials and full visibility. It says there are no hardcoded secrets or standing privileges and that every action is policy-enforced, encrypted and auditable.

Keeper Secrets Manager adds zero-knowledge secret delivery, automated credential rotation and protection for Non-Human Identities. The long-tail buyer question is: how do you audit Keeper AI-agent access from JIT secret to recorded session?

Five first-party evidence wedges

  1. AI-agent-native access: the named Secure AI Agents surface covers time-limited privileges, specific roles and access scopes.
  2. Ephemeral credential delivery: Keeper says JIT secrets are never written to disk, logs or prompts.
  3. Ephemeral remote access: encrypted tunnels grant access without VPNs, open ports or standing privileges.
  4. Session and command visibility: agent-initiated sessions, commands and secret usage can be logged, recorded and linked to a specific AI identity.
  5. Government-grade posture: Keeper's security page attests FedRAMP High, GovRAMP High, SOC 2 Type 2, ISO 27001/27017/27018, HIPAA, PCI DSS, GDPR and CCPA coverage.

The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — AI identity to JIT credential

Capture tenant, environment, AI identity, role, access scope, credential reference, approval policy, grant timestamp and expiry in one replayable receipt.

Gap 2 — Secret delivery to agent action

Join Keeper Secrets Manager delivery to the exact agent run, command or system interaction without copying the secret into logs, prompts or evidence exports.

Gap 3 — Ephemeral tunnel to recorded session

Preserve tunnel ID, target resource, authentication event, session ID, command stream, recording ID, close reason and result hash.

Gap 4 — Rotation to revocation

Link old credential reference, time- or event-driven rotation trigger, replacement reference, rollback decision, deployment result and revocation event.

Gap 5 — Tenant boundary to compliance export

Prove that tenant, environment and AI-identity boundaries survive incident-response, SOC 2, ISO and FedRAMP evidence exports.

Practical 48-hour scope

Sample one AI identity, one policy, one JIT credential, one agent action, one ephemeral tunnel, one recorded session, one rotation and one revocation. Mark each join durable, inferred or absent.
