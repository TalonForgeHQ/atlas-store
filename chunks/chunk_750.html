Gap 1 — Questionnaire schema lineage and GRX-reused response inheritance

For each customer-side assessment, retain the questionnaire framework and version, question schema, GRX-cached response version, reuse window, customer-tenant cache key, reviewing analyst, and exported response hash. A GRX-cached response used as if it were a fresh issue must still trace back to the original submission.

  • Questionnaire framework and version tag (e.g. SIG Lite 2024 Q3)
  • Vendor and responding tenant IDs plus framework version
  • GRX cached-response reuse event, window, and reviewer's audit hook
  • Exported response hash and analyst evidence pull timestamp

Gap 2 — Vendor-risk-scoring reproducibility and rationalization

For every published vendor risk score, retain the per-control evidence trail, the reviewer rationalization, the framework mapping, the response version, and the score-input timestamp. A score that cannot be regenerated from the same evidence is not auditable.

  • Per-control evidence artifacts, control IDs, and framework mapping
  • Response version, attachments, and reviewer's rationale field
  • Score-input timestamp, score provenance, and reviewer identity
  • Tenant-scoped score snapshot and recompute checksum

Gap 3 — AI-Based Control Reviews evidence boundary

For each HyperTPRM or AI-Based Control Review event, retain the ingested evidence IDs, the LLM sub-processor model and version, the accept-or-reject decision, the rationale summary, the human-reviewer sign-off, and the resulting downstream platform state. The AI decision and the human sign-off are two distinct logged events.

  • Ingested evidence artifact IDs, framework, and version
  • LLM sub-processor model, version, and decision rationale
  • Human-reviewer sign-off, reviewer ID, and sign-off timestamp
  • Downstream controls panel status and any external ticket linkage

Gap 4 — GRX sub-processor, co-controller, and cross-tenant isolation

For every reused vendor response, retain the tenant-shard key, the data-residency path, the access tenant identity, the access event timestamp, and the published sub-processor and co-controller register entries. A response that crosses tenant boundaries must still prove the originating tenant's authority.

  • Tenant-shard key, residency region, and access tenant ID
  • Sub-processor and co-controller register entry plus policy version
  • Access event timestamp, requester, and rationale
  • Cross-tenant isolation test result and breakage-incident history

Gap 5 — Deletion cascade, retention rollback, and re-import proof

For every closed vendor, exited exchange, or rolled-back response, retain the retention-policy trigger, the deletion-cascade log, the checksummed delete event, and the re-import path that proves the re-issued response is fresh and not a phantom old record. A demonstrable reproduction matters more than a one-time export.

  • Retention-policy trigger, version, and authorized actor
  • Deletion-cascade log with per-system delete timestamps
  • Checksummed delete event and provable-delete receipt
  • Re-import path, replays-tested window, and freshness attestation

Five-question procurement test

  1. Can one questionnaire response be reproduced through framework version, GRX cache key, reuse window, and analyst?
  2. Can one vendor risk score be regenerated from control evidence, reviewer's rationale, and score-provenance record?
  3. Can one AI Control Review be traced from ingested evidence through LLM version and decision to a dated human sign-off and downstream panel state?
  4. Can one GRX reuse event be traced through tenant-shard key, residency, requester, sub-processor register entry, and isolation test?
  5. Can a deletion cascade and a re-import be reproduced from retention trigger through checksum to a freshness attestation?
