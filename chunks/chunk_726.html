The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — Conversation and Citation Provenance

Can Nabla pin the encounter, the Paris-vs-New-York-region processing boundary, the speech-model version, the per-clinician/per-specialty Nabla Copilot configuration, the inline citation, the redaction event, and the retention decision for every clinical conversation?

Without this chain, a health system cannot prove which source utterances supported a generated clinical artifact, and the KLAS-rated "up to 95% accuracy" claim cannot be tied to a specific output.

Gap 2 — Nabla Copilot Configuration + LLM Sub-Processor Lineage

For every diagnosis, medication, symptom, procedure, and plan statement, can the buyer replay the Nabla Copilot configuration version, the LLM sub-processor version (Nabla's choice of OpenAI / Anthropic / in-house / open-weight), the prompt or extraction policy version, the source span, the confidence, the citation, and the note-draft version?

The evidence should distinguish an original spoken statement from an inferred or normalized clinical concept, and should expose which LLM was used for which section of the note.

Gap 3 — Clinician Review and Human Oversight

Can Nabla export who, when, role, tenant, region, and identity assurance approved, edited, rejected, or added to a generated note, with a pre-review and post-review artifact pair?

This is the human-oversight record for HIPAA accountability, SOC 2 change evidence, ISO/IEC 42001 governance, and EU AI Act Article 14(4). The NEJM AI Trial report (Dec 5, 2025) and the Nabla clinical-leadership roster (Matthew Sakumoto MD + Ed Lee MD MPH) are the operational evidence that human-oversight exists; the procurement question is whether it is exported.

Gap 4 — EHR Write-Back and Patient-Safety Receipt

Can a health system tie the final clinician-approved note to the exact Nabla Connect integration (Epic / athenahealth / all-EHR), the encounter ID, the destination record, the write-back result, the rollback event, the duplicate-prevention rule, and the safety-exception receipt?

Clinical documentation evidence is incomplete if the downstream record mutation cannot be reconstructed. Nabla Connect is the named EHR-embedding product; the procurement question is what receipt Nabla exports per write-back.

Gap 5 — Deletion, Retention, and Sub-Processor Cascade

When a patient encounter is deleted or a model/sub-processor changes, can Nabla prove propagation across audio (note: KLAS-rated "no audio storage" claim must flow into the deletion-receipt), transcript, note draft, caches, audit logs, backups, and downstream EHR artifacts?

The receipt should include the request, the policy version, the region (US vs EU-Frankfurt), the LLM sub-processor, the deletion event, the completion timestamp, and any legally retained audit record.
