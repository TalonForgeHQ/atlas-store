Cerbos AI-agent authorization audit: policy to action

Cerbos's first-party About page describes an authorization management platform for every identity and decision, with fine-grained, contextual and continuous authorization across applications, gateways, workloads and AI agents.

The long-tail buyer question is: how do you audit Cerbos AI-agent authorization from identity and policy to downstream action?

Why Cerbos belongs in an AI-agent authorization comparison

Cerbos names AI agents alongside applications, gateways and workloads as authorization subjects. That makes the key evidence seam the decision boundary: what identity asked for access, what context was evaluated, and what the authorized system did next.

The first-party About page names Emre Baran as CEO and co-founder and says Cerbos was founded in 2021 by Emre Baran, Alex Olivier and Charith Ellawala.

Five first-party evidence wedges

  1. Identity-aware decisions: record the agent, workload, tenant and human-on-behalf-of identity.
  2. Contextual authorization: capture environment, purpose, risk and approval state at decision time.
  3. Policy provenance: preserve policy, condition and version identifiers for every allow or deny.
  4. Continuous control: show how changed policy and revocation affect the next action.
  5. Exportable replay: produce a redacted, tenant-safe evidence record for incident and compliance review.

The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — Agent identity to decision

Join tenant, agent identity, workload, user-on-behalf-of, tool, resource, requested action and decision ID.

Gap 2 — Context and policy version

Record the exact policy, condition, policy version and contextual attributes that produced the result.

Gap 3 — Approval and human override

Preserve approval state, reviewer identity and override reason as durable evidence rather than prompt text.

Gap 4 — Decision to downstream action

Connect the authorization result to the API, service, database, file or workflow action and its outcome.

Gap 5 — Revocation to export

Prove that revocation, changed relationships and cross-tenant boundaries survive replay and redacted export.

Practical 48-hour scope

Sample one AI agent, one allowed call, one denied call, one policy change and one revoked relationship. Mark each join durable, inferred or absent.
