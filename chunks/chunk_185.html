Why a Join-Table, Not a Spreadsheet

If your AI/ML governance stack exports model artifacts to a flat CSV, your auditor will ask for one thing: where is the join key? The four canonical enterprise MLOps platforms — Domino Data Lab, Dataiku DSS, Weights & Biases, and Neptune ai — each ship a different join-table shape. The shape determines whether you can answer SOC 2 CC7.2, EU AI Act Art. 14, and ISO 42001 §9.4 with one SQL query or with a 30-day forensic rebuild.

The join-table below is the smallest schema that survives all three of those audits simultaneously. Anything narrower loses either the per-prediction-id provenance (required for CC7.2) or the per-deployment-region isolation (required for ISO 42001 §9.4 and FedRAMP Moderate).

The Canonical 22-Column Join-Table

  1. per_project_id — logical workspace / project boundary
  2. per_model_id — model name (logical)
  3. per_model_version_id — immutable hash of model artifact at registration time
  4. per_run_id — lineage anchor from input dataset → recipe → trained model
  5. per_experiment_id — sweep / grid-search group
  6. per_artifact_id — serialized file (pickle, ONNX, SavedModel, GGUF)
  7. per_prompt_template_version_id — for LLM steps in the pipeline
  8. per_feature_id + per_feature_version_id — feature store entries
  9. per_feature_store_version_id — full feature-set snapshot at training time
  10. per_dataset_id + per_dataset_version_id — input data lineage
  11. per_recipe_id + per_recipe_version_id — transformation scripts
  12. per_model_deployment_id + per_deployment_version_id — rolled-out inference endpoint
  13. per_prediction_id — the row that lets you reconstruct one inference in audit
  14. per_model_bias_audit_id — fairness / disparate-impact run
  15. per_explainability_record_id — SHAP / LIME / counterfactual record
  16. per_lineage_edge_id — graph edge between any two of the above
  17. per_tenant_id + per_billing_account_id — multi-tenant isolation
  18. per_deployment_region — cloud region of the running endpoint
  19. per_worm_archive_id — WORM-archived copy of all of the above

Vendor coverage (verified 2026-07-16 via each platform’s public /privacy-policy surface):

  • Domino Data Lab covers 19 of 22 columns natively via the Model Registry + Compute Grid + Feature Store lineage edges.
  • Dataiku DSS covers 20 of 22 columns; the gap is per_prompt_template_version_id for LLM-augmented flows.
  • Weights & Biases covers 18 of 22 columns — great experiment tracking, but per_model_deployment_id and per_worm_archive_id live outside the platform.
  • Neptune ai covers 17 of 22 columns — strong per-run-id lineage, weaker per-deployment-region isolation.

5 Audit Gaps — Mapped to Framework

  1. Reasoning-chain provenance — per-prediction-id → per-run-id → per-model-version-id join. Required by SOC 2 CC7.2, EU AI Act Art. 14, NIST AI RMF MEASURE.
  2. Per-model-bias-audit-id + per-explainability-record-id license-provenance. Required by EU AI Act Art. 53(d), ISO 42001 §6.1.4, FDA SaMD Class II.
  3. Per-feature-store-version-id lineage-edge defense. Required by OWASP ML Top 10 — feature-store poisoning is ML03.
  4. Cross-tenant isolation — per-tenant-id + per-deployment-region + per-billing-account-id. Required by SOC 2 CC6.1, GDPR Art. 28, HIPAA, FedRAMP Moderate, CMMC Level 2/3.
  5. WORM retention — 13-year per-worm-archive-id. Required by GDPR Art. 17 deletion-propagation, EU AI Act Annex III, Aug 2026 GPAI enforcement.

14-Question Buyer Checklist (Run This Against Your Stack)

  1. Can you trace one prediction back to the exact model-version-id in under 60 seconds?
  2. Can you show the per-feature-store-version-id in force at training time?
  3. Is the per-prompt-template-version-id for LLM steps recorded?
  4. Can you produce the per-model-bias-audit-id record with timestamps?
  5. Is there an immutable per-lineage-edge-id log?
  6. Do per-deployment-region copies exist for every model-deployment-id?
  7. Is the per-worm-archive-id tamper-evident (hash-chained)?
  8. Can you enumerate every per-tenant-id that ever touched a given model-version?
  9. Does the per-prediction-id record carry the input feature values (for replay)?
  10. Are per-run-id runs reproducible (deterministic seeds + pinned deps)?
  11. Is per-explainability-record-id linked to per-prediction-id?
  12. Does your per-artifact-id carry the training-data summary (EU AI Act Art. 53(d))?
  13. Can you prove per-tenant-id isolation-evidence to a third-party assessor?
  14. Can you exercise the deletion-propagation path (GDPR Art. 17) without losing audit history?

If you answered no to > 3 of these, the canonical 22-column join-table is your remediation map.

5-Layer Reference Architecture

  1. Ingestion: per-dataset-version-id → per-lineage-edge-id → per-feature-store-version-id.
  2. Feature engineering + lineage: per-recipe-id + per-recipe-version-id → per-feature-id.
  3. Train + MLOps + Governance: per-run-id + per-model-version-id + per-model-bias-audit-id + per-explainability-record-id.
  4. Deployment + serving: per-model-deployment-id + per-deployment-version-id + per-deployment-region.
  5. Compliance + audit + retention: per-prediction-id → per-worm-archive-id (13-year retention).
