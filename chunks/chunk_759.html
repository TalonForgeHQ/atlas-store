Five buyer-facing joins the Anyscale audit scores

  1. Job/Service → Ray cluster → Kubernetes node receipt. Can the buyer export one record joining the Ray job or service ID, the underlying Ray cluster ID, the KubeRay Kubernetes namespace, the worker node host, the GPU allocation event, the start/end time, and the worker log URL? This is the join that turns a Ray platform into a procurement-grade workload lineage.
  2. Cluster → dedicated GPU pool isolation receipt. For a deployment using Anyscale's dedicated GPU pool, can Anyscale show which GPU family, region, zone, network policy, and tenant-isolation boundary handled the request? The audit covers the published H100, H200, A100, L4, and L40S families without assuming every family is available in every region. Multi-tenant isolation evidence is required because most enterprise buyers ask for proof that another tenant's job cannot read worker memory.
  3. Autoscaler → workload queue → headroom receipt. Can a Ray job's autoscaler event be tied to the workload queue depth, the requested GPU type, the headroom policy, the scale-up trigger, the scale-down trigger, and the final quiescence event? This join is what distinguishes a managed-Ray platform from a "self-serve cluster" — the queue and the headroom are the operational surface auditors actually care about.
  4. Workspaces → IDE → user-action receipt. For workloads edited inside Anyscale Workspaces (managed Jupyter/VSCode), can the buyer join the workspace session, the developer identity, the action type, the cell or file changed, the Ray job or service launched, and the audit-log entry? This join is the human-oversight evidence required by EU AI Act Article 14 for agent or model development.
  5. Export → deletion proof across Ray + KubeRay + object storage. Can a customer export the joined record and then prove deletion across Ray logs, the Ray cluster autoscaler history, the KubeRay CRDs (RayJob, RayService, RayCluster), the per-job object-storage checkpoints, the worker ephemeral disk, and backups? The expected receipt names each scope, completion time, exception, and sub-processor dependency.

Anyscale-specific appendices to the audit

A. Ray cluster, KubeRay, and Kubernetes boundary

Anyscale's first-party claims include "production-ready Ray" running on Kubernetes via KubeRay CRDs (RayJob, RayService, RayCluster). The audit must score whether the customer's Ray cluster manifest, the underlying Kubernetes namespace, and the IAM/RBAC role binding join in one exportable row, because Ray jobs launched outside the customer's RBAC scope are the most frequent procurement-review failure mode.

B. Multi-cloud and BYO-cloud

Anyscale supports customer-managed cloud deployments. The audit should distinguish Anyscale-managed GPU pools from BYO-cloud GPU pools because the DPA cascade differs: a managed pool carries the Anyscale DPA + sub-processor list; a BYO-cloud pool carries the customer's own DPA + their sub-processor list (which may include AWS, GCP, Azure, OCI, or CoreWeave).

C. Open-source Ray vs Anyscale-managed Ray

Ray is open-source under the Linux Foundation. Anyscale contributes upstream changes. The audit must distinguish audit scope: open-source Ray deployment carries only the customer's DPA (no Anyscale sub-processor presence); Anyscale Platform deployment carries the Anyscale DPA + the Anyscale sub-processor cascade.

Common audit failures (avoid these)

  • Treating "we use Ray" as Anyscale platform evidence. The audit must score the difference between open-source Ray + Anyscale Platform. Buyers reject audit reports that conflate the two.
  • Skipping the KubeRay CRD lineage. Auditors routinely fail audits because the export shows the Ray job ID but not the underlying Kubernetes namespace, RBAC binding, or worker node. The CRD lineage is the join that closes the audit.
  • Conflating multi-tenant GPU pooling with private clusters. Anyscale offers both; the DPA cascade is different and must be distinguished per cluster per workload.
  • Skipping EU AI Act Article 14 evidence for Workspaces. If the buyer uses Workspaces, the developer-session-to-Ray-job join is the operational record required by Article 14. Skip it and the audit fails on the EU buyer side.
