The five-question evidence-gap checklist

  1. Vendor Risk + Breach Risk + User Risk lineage replay. Can a chosen UpGuard rating update be traced back through its vendor_id, questionnaire_id, document_id, extraction_id, model_version, breach_signal_id, trust_exchange_cohort_id, and tenant?   2. Tenant + Trust Exchange cohort isolation. Can UpGuard demonstrate that one customer's Vendor Risk, Breach Risk, User Risk, Trust Exchange trust-portal records, and AI Security Center AI-use-case-inventory never cross into another tenant through a shared cohort, shared GCP-project boundary, or shared AI Policy Generator template cache? The evidence should pair tenant boundaries with Trust Exchange cohort partitioning, GCP-project isolation, AI-use-case-inventory scoping, integration consent, credential scope, and deletion records.
  3. AI Security Center workforce-AI-governance audit trail. Every AI Policy Generator invocation and every AI Security Center governance signal needs a per-AI-use-case-id, per-employee-id, per-policy-template-version, per-acknowledgment-timestamp, per-replay-window, and per-tenant-isolation record. EU AI Act Art. 50(1) requires AI-generation disclosure for every EU-resident AI-policy artifact; ISO/IEC 42001:2023 AIMS clause 8.4 requires per-AI-action operational records.
  4. AI Policy Generator + CRPM model-version lineage. For regulated buyers, can an AI Policy Generator output be replayed within the AI Act + NIST AI RMF window including prompt_template_version, model_version, scoring_version, evidence_export_timestamp, and output_anchor_hash? The replay must include the prompt-template version, model version, scoring version, evidence-export timestamp, and output-anchor hash that ties the output back to the underlying policy library.

NIS2 Art. 21(2)(d) vulnerability-handling and Art. 21(2)(e) signed-firmware require per-finding handling-state and per-integration signed-firmware receipts.
