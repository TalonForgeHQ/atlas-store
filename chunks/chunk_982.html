Why One Identity is not a duplicate

Teleport starts with cryptographic infrastructure identity. Delinea starts with a converged PAM control plane and StrongDM runtime authorization. Boundary starts with an OIDC identity-aware proxy and Vault credential broker. One Identity starts with an identity fabric that explicitly joins human and non-human lifecycle governance to PAM across SaaS, hybrid, and on-premises deployment models. Its unique audit question is whether one AI assistant's lifecycle, risk decision, vault event, and privileged session remain explainable across every deployment boundary.

Five audit gaps for AI-agent privileged access

  1. Agent lifecycle lineage. Join human sponsor, agent identity, model and prompt version, Identity Manager state, approval, and revocation.
  2. Safeguard session replay. Join vault credential, target, action stream, recording, policy version, risk score, and final state.
  3. Explainable decision evidence. Preserve the exact signals, rule version, anomaly score, and human override behind adaptive access.
  4. Deployment-boundary normalization. Normalize SaaS, single-tenant, multi-tenant, hybrid, and on-premises receipts without losing tenant or residency context.
  5. Failure-path parity. Retain denied, expired, retried, cancelled, orphaned, recording-failed, and emergency-access sessions like successful sessions.

Five non-overlapping wedges vs Teleport, Delinea, and Boundary

  1. Identity-fabric-first join across IGA, PAM, AD/Entra management, and AI-agent governance.
  2. Explicit first-party AI-assistant impersonation risk plus lifecycle controls and explainable decisions.
  3. Safeguard and Safeguard On Demand paired on-premises and SaaS product models.
  4. Single-tenant, multi-tenant, hybrid, SaaS, and on-premises deployment evidence in one vendor surface.
  5. UNIX identity consolidation and Active Roles AD/Entra administration beside privileged-session controls.
