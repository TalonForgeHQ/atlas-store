Why Sensely is the omnichannel conversational-engagement sibling

Hippocratic AI owns the safety-governed generative healthcare-agent conversation lane with 1,000+ agents, 200m+ patient interactions, and a 7,500+ US licensed clinician safety-testing network. Ada Health owns the diagnostic-adjacent symptom-assessment and care-navigation lane with 14M+ users, 34M+ symptom assessments, and a clinician-safety dataset. Sensely owns the avatar-led omnichannel member-engagement and backend service-execution wedge: a member enters through any of app, web, SMS, WhatsApp, Messenger, WeChat, Line, or Telegram, in any of 30+ languages, and the conversation resolves to a service-execution task that touches a payer/provider backend. The evidence challenge is joining each conversation to the correct member identity and consent, the active channel and modality, the NLP/intent/content version, the clinical-content source and triage output, the plan/policy/eligibility version, the operational task and backend receipt, the human escalation, and the Mediktor-era retention/deletion evidence. No conversational-health vendor in the cohort is built for both avatar-led omnichannel member engagement and downstream service execution at this scale, and that is exactly why Sensely is the canonical omnichannel sibling for payer/provider buyers who need to defend conversational engagement at the task-execution receipt level.

This is not a feature-count comparison. A buyer-ready avatar-led omnichannel conversational-health platform should reproduce why a specific service-execution task ran, which NLP/intent/content version produced it, which member consented to which channel and language, which clinical-content source supplied the triage output, which plan/policy/eligibility version authorized the execution, which Sensely backend receipt was issued, which human escalation followed, and which Mediktor-era retention/deletion event closed the audit window.

The five audit gaps

Gap 1 — Member Identity, Consent, and Channel/Language/Modality

Can Sensely pin the member identity, the consent capture (purpose, scope, retention, channel), the active channel (app, web, SMS, WhatsApp, Messenger, WeChat, Line, Telegram), the language (one of 30+), and the modality (avatar/text/voice) for every conversational-health event?

Without this chain, a payer or provider cannot prove which consent authorized which channel/language/modality for a specific conversation, and the first-party omnichannel + multilingual positioning cannot be tied to a specific member.

Gap 2 — Conversation/NLP/Content Version and Clinical-Content Source

For every conversational turn, triage decision, chronic-condition-monitoring check, and custom-conversation branch, can the buyer replay the conversation policy version, the NLP/intent classification version, the custom-NLP/intent-expansion rule, the linguistic-design support version, the clinical-content source (e.g. third-party clinical-content library, custom-curated content, content licensed to Mediktor-era), the confidence, the citation, and the conversation-draft version?

The evidence should distinguish a clinical-content-sourced triage output from a Sensely-authored or Mediktor-authored custom branch, and should expose which language and channel the content version was active for.

Gap 3 — Plan/Service Eligibility and Policy Version

For every service-execution task, enrollment/onboarding step, policy-administration action, alert dispatch, preference-capture event, and front-door navigation routing, can Sensely export the member's plan, the active service eligibility, the policy version, the region, the benefit tier, and the policy-administration rule?

This is the policy-versioning record for HIPAA accountability, SOC 2 change evidence, ISO/IEC 42001 governance, and EU AI Act Article 14(4). The first-party Front Door Navigator page positions enrollment/onboarding, policy administration, alerts, faster issue resolution, preference capture, and backend integration; the procurement question is whether each event exports its policy-version pin.

Gap 4 — Operational Task Execution and Backend Receipt

Can a payer or provider tie the final Sensely conversational outcome to the exact backend integration (CRM, claims, eligibility, case management, schedule), the execution state (success, failure, retry), the human escalation reason and destination, the SLA, the closure outcome, and the correction/rollback event?

Conversational-health evidence is incomplete if the downstream service-execution task cannot be reconstructed. The first-party Solutions page covers text-or-voice engagement in 30+ languages, symptom checker and triage, chronic condition monitoring, front-door smart navigation, service-execution integration, real-time insights, custom conversations, custom NLP/intent expansion, and linguistic-design support — each touchpoint needs its own backend-receipt evidence.

Gap 5 — Mediktor Acquisition-Era Retention, Deletion, and Migration Cascade

When a member invokes deletion, portability, or access rights, can Sensely prove the deletion propagation across the active Sensely tenant, the Mediktor acquisition-era data migration, the partner-system propagation, the backup cascade, the retention policy, the region, the sub-processor, and the legally retained audit record?

The receipt should include the deletion request, the Mediktor-era migration event, the region, the sub-processor, the completion event, and the downstream propagation. The first-party homepage says Sensely is now part of Mediktor — the procurement question is whether the retention/deletion evidence exports both pre-acquisition Sensely and post-acquisition Mediktor lineages.
