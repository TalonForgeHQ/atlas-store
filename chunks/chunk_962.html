AI Third-Party Risk Management Vendor Benchmark: 5 Evidence Gaps

Published July 23, 2026 · Atlas Audit Prep · long-tail keyword: AI third-party risk management vendor benchmark

SecurityScorecard, Bitsight, UpGuard, RiskRecon, and Black Kite each expose a different third-party cyber-risk substrate. A useful procurement comparison therefore tests evidence replay—not feature-count parity—across rating updates, portfolio decisions, AI-assisted actions, and regulatory exports.

Research basis: this benchmark consolidates the five completed vendor-specific evidence maps in the ai_third_party_risk_management cohort. It does not claim access to private controls and does not assert that any vendor fails a control.

Gap 1: vendor-portfolio lineage

Can an auditor select one vendor and replay onboarding, evidence collection, rating changes, privacy-risk changes, remediation, and final portfolio tier with stable vendor, tenant, source, model, and decision identifiers?

Gap 2: multi-platform handoffs

When a rating, FAIR calculation, questionnaire, threat-intelligence signal, or AI-assisted recommendation moves into procurement or incident response, does the receiving system preserve the source event, version, timestamp, actor, and reason?

Gap 3: cross-tenant and regional isolation

Can the provider demonstrate that ratings, model inputs, threat intelligence, generated policies, and evidence exports remained inside the correct customer tenant and regional boundary, including retries and asynchronous processing?

Gap 4: failure-path retention

Successful actions are not enough. A defensible trail retains failed, retried, overridden, rolled-back, and expired actions with the same identifiers and supporting evidence used by the successful path.

Gap 5: regulatory evidence export

Can the record be exported without screenshot reconstruction for SEC Form 8-K Item 1.05 incident analysis, NIS2 Articles 21 and 23, EU AI Act human-oversight and transparency review, ISO/IEC 42001 AIMS coverage, ISO/IEC 27001, and SOC 2 Type II?

Minimum replay schema

  • tenant_id, vendor_id, portfolio_id, and regional processing zone;
  • source feed, questionnaire, rating, privacy rating, FAIR model, and control-map versions;
  • AI model name/version, prompt hash, response hash, tool call, approval, override, and rollback identifiers;
  • SCF or named-framework control ID, control version, evidence SHA-256, and retention state;
  • incident materiality decision, disclosure-clock timestamp, exporter identity, and export hash.

How to run the benchmark

  1. Select one representative vendor event from each platform.
  2. Request the complete replay package, including failed and overridden states.
  3. Normalize the five packages into the minimum schema above.
  4. Score every required join red, amber, or green.
  5. Document which missing joins require screenshots, support tickets, or manual testimony.

What procurement should receive

The output should be a five-row comparison, the highest-priority missing receipts, one sample replay schema, and a publication checklist for the buyer's Trust Center or internal control library. The goal is not a generic vendor ranking; it is a reproducible account of which platform can export which evidence at which level of granularity.
