The five evidence joins

  1. Discovery to ownership: record repository, commit, detector version, secret reference, tenant, owner, and agent identity without copying secret material.
  2. Finding to remediation: attach the ticket, approval, remediation timestamp, replacement credential reference, and policy version.
  3. Runtime use: join the approved credential to workload, deployment, agent run, tool name, request ID, and result hash.
  4. Rotation to revocation: preserve old version, replacement version, rollback, and revocation events as one immutable chain.
  5. Tenant boundary to export: prove staging findings and production evidence cannot cross customer or environment boundaries.

What the review should replay

Sample one agent, one discovered secret reference, one source commit, one remediation ticket, one replacement, and one tool call. Redact values. The deliverable is a control-evidence graph showing which links are durable, which are inferred, and which need a new export or identifier.

Discovery receipt

Capture the repository, commit, detector version, finding ID, secret fingerprint, tenant, owning team, agent identity, and first-seen timestamp. A fingerprint proves continuity without duplicating the credential.

Remediation receipt

Join the finding to its ticket, reviewer, approval policy, replacement reference, completion time, and verification scan. Preserve failure paths when a remediation is rejected or incomplete.

Agent runtime receipt

Record workload identity, deployment, agent run, tool-call ID, request ID, policy version, and result hash. This makes a credential's approved use reviewable without exposing its value.

Rotation receipt

Link the retired version to the replacement, rollback decision, revocation event, and downstream deployment. A timestamp alone is not enough for an audit replay.

Cross-tenant receipt

Keep tenant, environment, and export identifiers in every join. The control should demonstrate that a staging finding cannot appear in a production or another customer's evidence package.
