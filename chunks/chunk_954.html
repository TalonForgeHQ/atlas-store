The five-question evidence-gap checklist

  1. Goal-to-outcome reconstruction. Can a selected Mooncamp goal be replayed across its initiative, owner, check-in, dashboard, business review, integration event, and final outcome? A defensible export should bind stable identifiers such as goal_id, initiative_id, checkin_id, dashboard_id, review_id, workspace_id, and integration_event_id.
  2. Change and approval lineage. Can every OKR change be attributed to the exact user, role, timestamp, prior value, approval, and downstream state? Quarterly snapshots alone are not enough when an auditor asks who changed a target midway through a cycle and which Business Review consumed the edited figure.
  3. Tenant and integration isolation. Can Mooncamp demonstrate that one customer's goals, comments, check-ins, and dashboards never cross into another workspace through MS Teams, Jira, or a custom integration? The evidence should pair tenant boundaries with connector consent, credential scope, webhook delivery, retry, and deletion records.
  4. Germany-hosted security replay. Mooncamp states that it uses Hetzner Cloud servers in a German data center, ISO 27001-certified server locations, TLS 1.2 encryption in transit, 256-bit encryption at rest, SSO, and GDPR controls. Can those statements be mapped to current configuration evidence, key-management records, subprocessor scope, incident logs, and a repeatable customer export?
  5. Pricing-to-control reconciliation. The public pricing page lists Essential at €7/user/month billed annually, Professional at €10, and a custom Enterprise plan with a 100-user minimum. Can procurement tie each contracted security, support, administration, and integration control to the exact plan and order form rather than a generic marketing page?
