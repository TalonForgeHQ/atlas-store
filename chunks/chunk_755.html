Gap 1 — Customer-cloud deployment lineage

Every release should bind the chosen cloud, account or project, region, network, service identity, repository, source commit, build image, dependency lock, environment, deployment, and promotion actor into one exportable record.

  • GCP, AWS, Azure, or Civo account and region identity
  • Repository, commit, build image, builder, and dependency-lock hash
  • Environment, service, deployment, promotion, and rollback identities
  • Customer-cloud network, service account, and policy snapshot

Gap 2 — Kata runtime, sandbox, and compute decisions

For each sandbox or AI workload, preserve the runtime class, image, snapshot, GPU or CPU allocation, scaling decision, regional placement, health check, restart, operator override, and completion status. A buyer should be able to distinguish platform automation from human intervention.

  • Kata-based runtime and sandbox-image identifiers
  • GPU or CPU type, allocation, quota, and utilization receipt
  • Snapshot, persistence, restart, scale, and region-placement events
  • Health-check result, automated action, and human override

Gap 3 — Tenant, preview-environment, and IDP boundaries

Northflank's multi-tenant application, preview-environment, and internal-developer-platform surfaces create useful isolation primitives. The evidence layer should prove which tenant, namespace, branch, environment, service, database, secret, and network owned every action.

  • Tenant, namespace, branch, preview environment, and IDP project
  • Service, database, persistent volume, secret, and network boundary
  • Creation, access, promotion, merge, expiry, and deletion timestamps
  • Cross-tenant denial and failed-access evidence

Gap 4 — Identity, access, and policy-change receipts

Each production-impacting action should join the authenticated person or workload identity to its role, tenant, cloud account, environment, approval, policy version, and resulting change. That join is what makes a deployment history defensible without manual correlation.

  • Human or service identity and authentication context
  • Role, environment permission, approval, and policy version
  • Public-networking, source, region, compute, and quota guardrails
  • Audit event, resulting state, and reviewer disposition

Gap 5 — Recovery, retention, and teardown proof

A replay is incomplete unless it can show what remained after failure or deletion. Preserve logs, metrics, snapshot restores, failed health checks, rollbacks, retained images, persistent volumes, secret rotations, customer-cloud resources, and final deletion completion.

  • Logs, metrics, utilization, failed-health-check, and recovery evidence
  • Snapshot or preview-environment restore and rollback receipt
  • Retained image, artifact, database, volume, and secret inventory
  • Sandbox, deployment, and customer-cloud resource deletion receipt
