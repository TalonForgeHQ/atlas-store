What changed in 2024 and why it matters

Defined.ai was founded in 2018 by Daniela Braga in Seattle, WA. Through 2023 the company sold annotation services directly. In 2024 it pivoted to a marketplace model: contributors list datasets, Defined.ai hosts the catalog, license templates, and Diana (a chatbot for discovery), and buyers accept license terms at checkout. The pivot is meaningful for buyers because the audit chain now spans three parties, not two.

Daniela Braga's public record — ex-Microsoft, ex-Facebook, ex-Amazon Alexa ML Lead, Forbes AI luminary, TIME 100 AI 2024 nominee, White House AI working-group participant, and US Congressional testimony on AI ethics — adds governance credibility that other marketplace vendors do not carry. Use that signal: a marketplace with a founder willing to testify publicly about AI ethics has a higher floor for documentation quality than one without.

EU AI Act Art. 53(d) GPAI readiness

Defined.ai's marketplace touches the EU AI Act Art. 53(d) GPAI training-data transparency obligation because buyers can ingest marketplace datasets into GPAI-class models. The documentation target is Aug 2 2026. Ask Defined.ai for:

  • A per-listing training-data transparency summary (Art. 53(d))
  • A per-dataset copyright-summary record (Art. 53(1)(b))
  • A per-contributor license-chain record
  • A per-tenant KMS-key isolation matrix (SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + Schrems II SCC + EU-US DPF)
  • A WORM retention policy that covers contributor-payout records, license-royalty records, and Diana prompt traces

When to use Defined.ai vs the alternatives

Use Defined.ai when you need license-cleared datasets quickly and you want a vendor with public AI-ethics governance credibility. Use Labelbox when you want to own the label stack internally. Use Scale AI or Surge AI when you need premium RLHF for a frontier-model release. Use Appen when you need multilingual coverage across 130+ countries. Use Snorkel when you have strong engineering capacity and want labeling functions instead of a managed service. Use Innodata when you need regulated-industry depth (banking, legal, medical, publishing).

Watch-out: a marketplace license is only as good as its contributor-side evidence. Ask Defined.ai for the contributor KYC process, the source-asset provenance chain, and the contributor-payout audit log. If they cannot produce these in writing, the license is unverified at the contributor end and your downstream use may be exposed.
