The Five Audit Questions

1. One Contract-Action Receipt Across Six Agent Surfaces

Can a single contract_action_receipt reconstruct contract ID and version, Legal-Grade agent (Draft, Negotiate, Analyze, Comply, Investigate, or Collaborate), prompt and policy versions, proprietary model or Mixture-of-Experts route, precedent/source hash, recommendation, lawyer decision, downstream action, and final repository or obligation state? This is the core EU AI Act Article 12, ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, and SOC 2 CC7.2 evidence chain.

2. Tenant-Specific Proof for Dedicated AWS Isolation

Luminance's security page states that each customer receives a dedicated single-tenant AWS instance with no co-mingling, AES-256 encryption through AWS KMS, TLS 1.2 or higher in transit, nightly backups to a secondary data center in the same region, and at least 14 days of backup retention. The auditable artifact should join tenant, region, KMS key version, S3/EC2 encryption state, access grant, backup object, restore test, retention window, and deletion receipt.

3. Clause-Level Provenance for AI-Powered Checklists

AI-Powered Checklists combine Lumi Summary, Lumi Generate, Legal-Grade reasoning, preferred/fallback positions, internal benchmarks, and Microsoft Word mark-up. For every changed clause, the evidence packet should preserve checklist version, clause/source hash, benchmark version, model route, stated reason, policy threshold, lawyer override, accepted redline, and final signed-clause hash. Without that chain, a dashboard cannot prove why a material contract position changed.

4. Licensed-Professional Human Oversight

Every material recommendation should carry the supervising lawyer, bar-admission jurisdiction, ABA Model Rule 1.1 competence control, Model Rule 1.6 confidentiality/privilege classification, Model Rule 5.5 unauthorized-practice guardrail result, applicable UK SRA or EU Member State Bar rule, and the time-stamped human acceptance or rejection rationale. This converts “human in the loop” into an EU AI Act Article 14(4) machine-readable record.

5. Investigation and eDiscovery Legal-Hold Reconstruction

Luminance's Investigate surface creates a distinct legal-hold obligation. A defensible export should join source-document hash, corpus and cluster versions, search/query, responsiveness result, privilege classification, human override, export set, hold status, deletion block, downstream disclosure, and retention receipt under FRCP 26 and 37. That is the highest-value audit wedge absent from the Harvey and Spellbook siblings.
