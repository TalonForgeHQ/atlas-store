Why AI data-labeling + RL-platform demands a distinct audit lane

Labelbox powers the data factory for frontier-model labs and enterprise-specialist-agent buyers: a 2.6M+ Alignerr network produces human-preference annotations, RLHF preference-decisions, eval-benchmark decisions, and Recursion RL-training-steps. Each annotation is a human judgment that downstream RL agents will inherit. A procurement reviewer therefore needs more than a generic SaaS security page: they need evidence joining labeler identity, task design, annotation lineage, preference-decision provenance, and tenant isolation into a single auditable row.

This checklist targets the long-tail questions buyers search for: Labelbox vendor audit, Alignerr labeler consent provenance, RLHF preference-data poisoning defense, EU AI Act GPAI training-data documentation, and Recursion RL-platform procurement due diligence.

Seven questions to ask before approving an AI data-labeling + RL-platform vendor

  1. Is every annotation joined to a labeler identity and consent record? Require annotation_id, labeler_id, labeler_consent_event_id, labeler_compensation_record_id, labeler_demographic_bucket_id (separate-statistical-purpose), tenant_id, and labeler_withdrawal_event_id in one exportable row.
  2. Is every RLHF preference-decision joined to its annotation lineage? Distinguish per-preference preference_decision_id, prompt_id, completion_a_id, completion_b_id, labeler_id, task_id, rubric_version_id, model_version_id, tenant_id, with preference_timestamp and residency_region_id.
  3. Can the platform defend against labeler-poisoning + preference-data-poisoning? Require per-labeler statistical-baseline monitoring, per-task disagreement-distribution anomaly detection, golden-task sanity checks, and a named reviewer for any preference-decision that exceeds a configurable trust threshold. Tie the defense to OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01/LLM06 and EU AI Act Art. 14 human oversight.
  4. Does the Recursion RL-training-step inherit GPAI documentation obligations? Each Recursion RL-training-step must carry gpaio_training_data_summary_id linking to the EU AI Act Art. 53(1)(b) copyright-summary + Art. 53(d) training-data-transparency record. The buyer needs to walk from training-step → preference-decisions → annotations → labelers → consent events in one query.
  5. Are tenants isolated through the full RL-training path? Check labeler pools, task routing, annotation storage, preference-decision caches, RL-training-run clusters, eval-benchmark isolates, and KMS keys — not only the primary database — against SOC 2 CC6.1, GDPR Art. 28, and Schrems II.
  6. Can the vendor honor labeler-consent withdrawal? A consent-withdrawal event must propagate to the labeler_id (no new tasks), to existing annotations (flag + tombstone without retention of the human judgment), to RL-training-datasets (purge from training queue + flag completed models), to eval-benchmarks (purge), and to backup-expiry records. Preserve an auditable tombstone without retaining the personal annotation.
  7. Who approved the RLHF preference-decision for the intended downstream use? Link each published preference-decision to human_approval_id, approver_role, downstream_model_id, intended_buyer, intended_jurisdiction, and rollback_record_id for SOC 2 CC7.2, EU AI Act Art. 14, and 12-state AI-disclosure evidence.

Join-table 4 — cross-tenant Annotate + Model + Catalog + Recursion isolation

This is the SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + Schrems II backbone. The buyer needs to verify that tenant A's labeler-pool, annotation storage, preference-decision caches, RL-training-run clusters, eval-benchmark isolates, and KMS keys are physically or cryptographically separated from tenant B's. A single shared labeler-pool across financial-services and consumer-tenants is a procurement-disqualifying finding. The evidence must include a per-tenant KMS-key rotation log + a per-tenant labeler-pool membership log + a per-tenant RL-training-run-isolation log.

Columns: tenant_id, labeler_pool_id, annotation_storage_partition_id, preference_decision_cache_partition_id, rl_training_run_cluster_id, eval_benchmark_isolate_id, kms_key_id, kms_key_rotation_event_id, residency_region_id, audit_log_entry_id.
