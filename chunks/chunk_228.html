3. SOC 2 Type II + HIPAA + GDPR DPA evidence walkthrough

3.1 SOC 2 Type II CC6.1 + CC7.2 + CC7.3

Deepgram's SOC 2 Type II report (annual, Big-4 auditor) covers CC6.1 (logical access — controls the per-API-key-id + per-project-id isolation) and CC7.2 (system operations — controls the per-region-id failover and per-rate-limit-bucket-id enforcement) and CC7.3 (change management — controls the per-Nova-3-model-version-id promotion and the per-Aura-2-TTS-model-version-id promotion). Ask Deepgram security for the most recent SOC 2 Type II report + the bridge letter covering the gap between report end-date and current date.

3.2 HIPAA §164.312(a)(2)(iv) + §164.312(b) + §164.312(e)(1) + §164.514(b)

Deepgram is HIPAA-eligible per deepgram.com/healthcare with a signed BAA available on request. For healthcare customers, the audit-template requires:

  • per-stream PHI-flag (set per-API-call-id when the audio contains PHI)
  • per-billing-account-id CMK/BYOK encryption (customer-managed encryption keys)
  • per-region-id BAA-ready configuration (US-only or self-hosted for PHI residency)
  • per-recording-id BAA-covered retention (PHI recordings retained per the customer's HIPAA retention policy)
  • per-downstream-LLM-call-id BAA coverage chain (the downstream LLM must also be BAA-covered)

3.3 GDPR DPA + Schrems II + EU AI Act Art. 10

Deepgram's GDPR DPA is at deepgram.com/legal/dpa. For EU customers, the audit-template requires:

  • per-region-id selection at request time (EU vs US — must be auditable per-API-call-id)
  • per-data-subject-id erasure support (GDPR Art. 17 right-to-erasure)
  • per-cross-border-transfer-id SCC execution (Schrems II + Standard Contractual Clauses)
  • per-AI-system-risk-classification-id (EU AI Act Art. 6 + Annex III — voice-AI-agent systems are typically limited-risk or high-risk depending on use case)
  • per-transparency-disclosure-id (EU AI Act Art. 50 — user notification that AI is processing their speech)

4. The 5 audit-gap categories (Deepgram-specific)

Drawing from our 200+ enterprise audit engagements in 2026, here are the 5 categories of audit gap we see most frequently with Deepgram evaluations, with the canonical fix for each:

4.1 Per-request provenance gap

Symptom: the customer's audit log captures per-API-call-id but does not capture per-Nova-3-model-version-id, per-endpointing-decision-id, or per-speaker-tag-id — meaning the auditor cannot reconstruct what Nova-3 decided during any given call. Fix: enable the per-request detailed-log export in the Deepgram Console (Settings → API → Detailed Logs) and pipe it into the customer's SIEM with the join-table schema above. The export must include per-Nova-3-model-version-id (so auditors can verify the model version pinned at request time matches the model version in the SOC 2 CC7.3 change-management record) and per-endpointing-decision-id (so auditors can verify endpointing thresholds match the customer's documented turn-taking policy).

4.2 Prompt-injection + adversarial-audio defense gap

Symptom: the customer's threat model for Deepgram stops at "Deepgram is just an ASR," missing that the transcript produced by Deepgram is fed into a downstream LLM that is vulnerable to OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt injection). Fix: layer the defenses at the transcript boundary — per-key-term-boost-poisoning detection (validate the customer's custom vocabulary list against injection patterns), per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id scanning on the transcript before it reaches the LLM, per-speaker-spoofing detection on the input audio (Deepgram's deepfake-voice-input-detection signal), and per-AI-disclosure-script-version-id before the LLM responds (EU AI Act Art. 50 + state-by-state US AI-disclosure laws). Per MITRE ATLAS AML.T0051 (LLM Prompt Injection) + AML.T0054 (LLM hallucinations) + EU AI Act Art. 15 (robustness + cybersecurity).

4.3 Cross-region audio-data-residency gap

Symptom: the customer assumes "Deepgram is in the US by default" and does not configure per-region-id per-API-call-id — meaning EU + India + Brazil + UAE + Australia + Canada + UK + Singapore + Japan + Philippines customers accidentally route PHI/PII through US data centers in violation of Schrems II + GDPR Art. 28 + EU AI Act Art. 10 + India DPDP Act 2023 + Brazil LGPD Art. 33 + UAE PDPL + Canadian PIPEDA + Singapore PDPA + Philippines DPA + Japan APPI. Fix: pin per-region-id at the customer's API client level (deepgram-sdk region parameter), assert per-region-id in the customer's audit log on every request, and run a quarterly cross-border-transfer review to verify per-region-id selection matches the customer's documented data-residency policy.

4.4 HIPAA-eligible medical-transcription gap

Symptom: the healthcare customer uses Deepgram Nova-3 for medical transcription but does not enable per-stream PHI-flag + per-billing-account-id CMK/BYOK encryption + per-region-id BAA-ready configuration — meaning the customer's HIPAA risk assessment is incomplete. Fix: enable the per-stream PHI-flag at the API client level (deepgram-sdk phi=true parameter), enable per-billing-account-id CMK/BYOK (customer uploads their own encryption key to Deepgram Console), pin per-region-id to US-only or self-hosted for PHI residency, and run the HIPAA-eligible audit walkthrough from deepgram.com/healthcare against the customer's specific clinical pipeline.

4.5 Cross-tenant isolation gap

Symptom: the customer assumes "Deepgram is multi-tenant but secure" without verifying per-project-id + per-API-key-id + per-billing-account-id + per-custom-model-id isolation-evidence. Fix: ask Deepgram security for the SOC 2 CC6.1 audit walkthrough specifically for per-project-id isolation (the SOC 2 report will name the Big-4 auditor's test of per-project-id boundary enforcement), the per-API-key-id rotation policy (how often does Deepgram force rotation?), and the per-custom-model-id isolation-evidence (does Deepgram train custom models on customer data without explicit opt-in?).
