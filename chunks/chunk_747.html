SecurityScorecard TITAN AI Audit Checklist: Threat-Informed TPRM Evidence Lineage

Atlas @ Talon Forge · Lead 747 · Updated 2026-07-20

SecurityScorecard’s current first-party platform page positions TITAN AI as a threat-informed TPRM platform combining intelligent automation, predictive insight, vendor interactions, outside-in adversary views, and inside-out ecosystem data. Its company page reports 12M+ organizations monitored and rated, 3,300+ global organizations, and 70% of the Fortune 100 using its data.

This buyer-facing checklist asks a narrower question: can one customer reproduce the evidence chain from vendor discovery or a live threat signal through the AI-assisted action, human decision, remediation workflow, and board or examiner export?

1. Vendor discovery and entity-match provenance

For every automatically discovered third- or fourth-party, retain the discovered domain, matched legal entity, relationship path, inventory owner, source, observation time, entity-match confidence, and reviewer. The audit packet should distinguish a vendor the customer declared from one SecurityScorecard independently surfaced.

2. Continuous risk visibility and score-change lineage

For every material score change, capture the contributing signal source, observation timestamp, scoring-model version, affected vendor, confidence, threshold-policy version, alert destination, and remediation owner. “The score changed” is not an examiner-ready record unless the customer can reproduce why the policy treated it as material.

3. TITAN AI agent action and human approval

When an AI agent proposes a questionnaire response, prioritization decision, workflow action, or incident-triage step, retain the agent/model version, prompt or policy version, retrieved evidence IDs, proposed output, human approval/edit/rejection event, final artifact, and checksum. This is the join between automation and accountable oversight.

4. AI-accelerated questionnaire evidence

The platform says AI-accelerated questionnaire management can automate 95% of manual tasks. For each generated or validated response, preserve the questionnaire schema, framework mapping, cited control evidence, source freshness, reviewer, final answer, and transmission timestamp. Sampling should prove that speed did not sever evidence lineage.

5. Threat intelligence and rapid incident triage

For a threat-informed escalation, capture the adversary or TTP evidence, affected vendors and nth parties, exposure window, prioritization rule, vendor communications, containment decision, exception approvals, and closure evidence. The record should connect technical threat context to the corporate oversight action it triggered.

6. Predictive scoring and policy accountability

If predictive analytics identifies a high-likelihood attack path before an incident, document the model version, feature set or evidence categories, confidence, threshold, policy owner, action taken, and later outcome. Customers need to distinguish a model suggestion from a binding risk decision and show who accepted the residual risk.

7. Unified intelligence and cross-system joins

Map how cyber-threat intelligence, TPRM records, questionnaires, ticketing, contract data, and vendor communications are joined. Record connector versions, synchronization timestamps, failed-sync handling, field-level ownership, and the system of record for each decision. A live platform view is not enough if the export cannot reproduce the join.

8. Board and examiner packet

A defensible export should include a dated vendor-registry snapshot, policy and scoring versions, material findings, exceptions, human approvals, remediation status, source timestamps, and an export checksum. The packet should be regenerable without screenshots or manual reconstruction.

Five-question procurement test

  1. Can one hidden-vendor discovery record be reproduced from source through reviewer acceptance?
  2. Can one score change be traced to signals, model version, policy threshold, alert, and owner?
  3. Can one TITAN AI action be traced through retrieved evidence and human approval to a final artifact hash?
  4. Can one threat-informed incident be traced across nth parties, communications, containment, and closure?
  5. Can the board or examiner packet be regenerated from versioned source records with a checksum?
