Why Zep's GraphRAG + auto-summarization substrate is cohort-unique

Zep turns a long conversation into a graph of nodes and edges plus a structured fact table, then selects one of four buffer types (Message Window + Summary + Token + Perpetual) when re-injecting context into the next model call. The auto-summary and fact-extraction events fire as part of the buffer selection, not after. Procurement therefore needs a receipt that joins the buffer decision to the graph state to the fact table to the LLM call, not three separate audit logs. Buffer selection is a regulated decision, not an internal optimization

Zep's auto-summarization fires when the conversation crosses a threshold. Which buffer is selected changes what the next model call sees. A fact that was added in turn 4 and not re-summarized into the summary buffer by turn 12 is invisible to the model unless Zep chose the Message Window or Perpetual buffer. The receipt must capture which buffer was selected, not just that some context was injected. None of the five cohort vendors expose the buffer-selection decision in their default audit logs. The gap map names Zep as the vendor that gets closest, with PARTIAL coverage on the buffer selection and full coverage on the fact table.

GraphRAG edges are a first-class audit surface

When a fact is extracted into Zep's structured fact table, the receipt must include the GraphRAG edge identity that connects the new fact to its source conversation turn and to the user. The cross_user_no_bleed audit trail then proves that a fact extracted in user A's session is not visible in user B's session, even when both sessions share the same underlying embedding index. SOC 2 CC7.2 + GDPR Art. 17 + EU AI Act Art. 12 all depend on this audit trail existing and being exportable per request.
