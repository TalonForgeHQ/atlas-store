1. Why AI whiteboards are squarely inside Article 53(d) GPAI scope

The EU AI Act's Article 53 lists the obligations for providers of "general-purpose AI models" placed on the EU market after 2 August 2025. Article 53(1)(b) imposes a copyright-and-training-data summary obligation; Article 53(d) imposes the technical-documentation obligation under Annex XI; Annex XI in turn requires providers to supply the Commission, on request, with information about the model's capabilities, limitations, and risk-management posture. The cross-sibling ai_whiteboard cohort qualifies because each vendor now ships a general-purpose model that is integrated into a collaborative surface and is exposed to enterprise customers across the EU: Miro AI Agents + Miro AI Platform, FigJam Jambot, Mural AI + Mural AI Sidekick, Conceptboard AI Brainstorm / AI Cluster / AI Summarize / AI Generate, and Lucidspark Collaborative AI + Auto-Summarize + AI Cluster. None of them are niche vertical tools — they are GPAI surfaces consumed by regulated EU buyers.

  2. Model card + capabilities + limitations disclosure. Required by Art. 53(d) + Annex XI §1. Verify the vendor publishes a model card naming the underlying foundation model (OpenAI / Anthropic / Google / Mistral / open-source) and the per-feature model-version pinning.   3. Training-data summary (Art. 53(1)(b)). Required by 2 August 2026. The summary must be published in a machine-readable form per the Commission's template. Verify the vendor exposes the public training-data-summary URL on the trust / security page. None of the five has published the Art. 53(1)(b) template as of 2026-07-19 — this is the largest single compliance gap in the cohort.
  4. Risk-management system (Art. 53(d) + Annex XI §2). Must be documented as a Plan-Do-Check-Act loop covering identification, evaluation, mitigation, and post-market monitoring. Verify the vendor publishes ISO 42001 (AI management system) certification. Cohort state: Miro → ISO 42001:2023 verified on miro.com/trust/; Lucidspark → AI-governance framework named on lucid.co/security but ISO 42001 certification not yet publicly listed (gap); Conceptboard → AI-governance framework named on conceptboard.com/security but ISO 42001 not yet publicly listed (gap); Mural → AI-governance framework named on mural.com/security but ISO 42001 not yet publicly listed (gap); FigJam → inherits Figma ISO 42001 (gap-flagged in Figma 582 evidence file).
  5. Human-oversight design (Art. 14). The AI features that mutate a board LIVE during a workshop must have a documented human-approval gate. Verify the vendor's AI-agent feature flow has a publish/confirm step before cluster writes, mind-map writes, action-item drafts, and diagram generations become durable board state. Miro AI Agents have explicit human-confirm steps (miro.com/ai/); Mural AI Sidekick has explicit human-confirm steps (mural.com/ai/); Lucidspark AI Cluster / AI Mind-Map have explicit human-confirm steps (lucid.co/figjam AI); FigJam Jambot has explicit human-confirm steps (figma.com/figjam/); Conceptboard AI Brainstorm has explicit human-confirm steps (conceptboard.com/ai/).
  6. Transparency obligation (Art. 50). The vendor must disclose that the content was AI-generated. Verify the board audit-log entry tags AI-mutated objects with a `source=ai` attribute. Cohort state: Miro → audit-log tags `source=ai` on Miro AI Agents writes; Mural → audit-log tags `source=ai` on Mural AI mutations; Lucidspark → audit-log tags `source=ai` on Lucid AI mutations; Conceptboard → audit-log tags `source=ai`; FigJam → audit-log tags `source=ai` (per figma.com/security).
  7. Copyright compliance (Art. 53(1)(c)). The provider must put in place a copyright-compliance policy. Verify the vendor has an opt-out mechanism for training-data scraping and a takedown workflow. Cohort state: all five have copyright policies on their respective /legal/ pages; the opt-out mechanism specifically for AI-training is the open question (gap).
  8. Data-residency + Schrems II SCC posture. Required for any EU customer running regulated workloads. Cohort state: Miro → EU data residency + Schrems II SCC + EU-US DPF + UK Extension + Swiss-US DPF (miro.com/trust/); Mural → EU data residency + Schrems II SCC + EU-US DPF (mural.com/security/); Conceptboard → EU-only data residency + DSGVO + Schrems II SCC + EU-US DPF + UK Extension + Swiss-US DPF (conceptboard.com/security/); Lucidspark → Schrems II SCC + EU-US DPF (lucid.co/security); FigJam → inherits Figma Schrems II SCC + EU data residency.

4. The single highest-leverage gap across the cohort
