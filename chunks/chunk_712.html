Why Hyperproof Is a Distinct Compliance-Automation Sibling

Drata anchors continuous evidence collection and framework automation. Thoropass joins readiness work to expert audits and report issuance. Hyperproof adds a central controls-and-risk operating layer. Its distinct audit wedge is whether control mappings, risk dependencies, evidence objects, issues, and approvals remain joined as frameworks and operating conditions change.

This is not a feature-count comparison. A buyer-ready GRC system should reproduce why a control satisfied a specific requirement at a specific time, which risks depended on it, which evidence supported it, who reviewed it, and what changed later.

The Five Audit Gaps

Gap 1 — Control-to-Framework Lineage

For every control, can Hyperproof export the framework and requirement version, mapping-rule version, inherited scope, control owner, evidence dependencies, reviewer, exception, approval, and effective timestamp?

When a framework version changes, the receipt should identify which mappings were preserved, replaced, or invalidated rather than silently updating a completion score.

Gap 2 — Risk-to-Control Dependency

When a risk score, treatment, or owner changes, can the platform reconstruct the prior state, scoring-method version, affected controls, linked evidence, treatment decision, approver, timestamp, and downstream framework impact?

The defensible unit is a state-change receipt, not the current risk-register row.

Gap 3 — Evidence Provenance and Freshness

Every evidence object should preserve source integration, source object ID, collection time, checksum, retention state, control mapping, reviewer decision, stale-evidence trigger, and replacement lineage.

If an integration or source object changes, buyers should be able to identify every control, risk, issue, and report that relied on the previous version.

Gap 4 — Issue-to-Remediation Closure

For every failed control or audit issue, can one receipt join the finding, severity method, affected requirement, remediation owner, due date, ticket or change, compensating control, retest result, exception rationale, and human sign-off?

This closes the common gap between a GRC finding, an engineering ticket, and the evidence that proves remediation.

Gap 5 — Point-in-Time Buyer Export

Can a machine-readable bundle reproduce the exact control, risk, evidence, issue, policy, reviewer, framework, and point-in-time state behind every claim without a spreadsheet reconciliation step?

The export should preserve stable identifiers and version joins so an auditor or procurement team can replay the claim after the live workspace has changed.
