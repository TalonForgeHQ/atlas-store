Eight questions to ask before approving an enterprise UX-research platform

  1. Can every recording be joined to contributor consent? Require contributor_id, consent_event_id, biometric_consent_event_id, recording_id, research_study_id, tenant_id, and residency_region_id in one exportable row.
  2. Is biometric data captured under explicit consent? Distinguish video-recording-consent, screen-recording-consent, facial-expression-consent, voice-recording-consent, and AI-summary-consent. Record the consent-policy version, exception decision, and withdrawal timestamp for every recording segment.
  3. Is sensitive data removed before AI-summary access? Distinguish capture-time masking, transcription-time redaction, EnjoyHQ-summary filtering, and human overrides. Record the redaction-policy version and exception decision for every segment sent to a model.
  4. Can every AI summary or tag cite its source? Join ai_summary_id, ai_tag_id, ai_sentiment_id, enjoyhq_answer_id to the exact transcript segment, recording timestamp, model version, prompt version, retrieval query, and cited clip.
  5. What blocks prompt injection through contributor speech? Contributor transcripts and uploaded notes are untrusted content. Require input delimiting, tool restrictions, retrieval filtering, output validation, and human approval under OWASP LLM01 and LLM06 + EU AI Act Art. 14.
  6. Are customers isolated throughout research and AI processing? Check recordings, transcripts, vector indexes, AI summaries, caches, evaluation datasets, exports, model gateways, and User Interviews contributor-pool routing against SOC 2 CC6.1 and GDPR Art. 28.
  7. Does deletion propagate everywhere? A contributor withdrawal must reach the recording, transcript, AI summary, AI tag, embedding, EnjoyHQ repository, evaluation copy, export, and backup-expiry record across all 4 UserTesting products.
  8. Who approved a research conclusion? Link every report export, product recommendation, enterprise-research deliverable, and stakeholder summary to human_approval_id, purpose, timestamp, and rollback record.

Five failure modes buyers should test

  • A contributor withdraws biometric consent, but a facial-expression fragment remains in an EnjoyHQ AI-summary vector index or evaluation dataset.
  • A health detail, customer name, email address, or confidential roadmap item reaches the EnjoyHQ AI-summary model before redaction.
  • An adversarial sentence in a contributor transcript changes the AI summary or causes retrieval of another tenant's research.
  • An EnjoyHQ repository answer presents a theme without source segments, timestamps, or contrary evidence.
  • A generated research recommendation reaches a downstream enterprise-research workflow without a named reviewer or approval timestamp.
