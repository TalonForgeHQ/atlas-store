If you're building AI-native products on a tight budget and need self-hostable observability, pick Helicone. If you're an enterprise Fortune-500 with deep pockets, pick Arize AI. For SOC 2 CC7.2 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + ISO 42001 9.4 + OWASP LLM Top 10 reasoning-chain reconstruction, you need the join-table to capture at minimum: per-request-id + per-prompt-id + per-completion-id + per-model-version-id + per-tenant-id + per-deployment-region + per-API-key-id. Helicone 7/7 — open-source wins the baseline

Helicone-Proxy + Helicone-Dashboard + Helicone-Edge + Cloudflare-Workers-Deploy gives you the full per-request-id → per-deployment-region trace with per-tenant-id isolation. Self-hostable means the audit data never leaves your VPC. This is why OSS-first enterprise teams default to Helicone.

FAQ

Is Helicone really open-source?

Yes — github.com/Helicone/helicone is Apache-2.0 licensed with 5K+ GitHub stars and active contributors. You can self-host via Docker + K8s, deploy Helicone-Edge to Cloudflare Workers, or use the hosted SaaS at helicone.ai.

Does Arize have an open-source offering?

Yes — Phoenix (github.com/Arize-ai/phoenix) is OSS for LLM evals + tracing + drift. The enterprise Arize AI platform is closed-source SaaS with Phoenix integration.

Does Patronus have an open-source offering?

Partial — Patronus-Evaluator-SDK is OSS but the production platform + Patronus-Production-Trace-Explorer + Patronus-Conversation-Explorer + Custom-Evaluator library are SaaS-only.

Can I use all three together?

Yes — many production teams ship Helicone for cost-tracking + caching, Arize-Phoenix for drift + tracing, and Patronus for agentic-evaluation + hallucination-detection. The audit-trail surfaces are complementary, not redundant.
