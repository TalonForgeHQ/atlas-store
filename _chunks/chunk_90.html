The 14-col RDU provenance join-table (SambaNova)

SOC 2 CC7.2 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + ISO 42001 9.4 require an end-to-end join-table per RDU inference call. Reference schema:

CREATE TABLE rdu_inference_provenance (
  inference_call_id        UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  tenant_id                UUID NOT NULL,
  host_cpu_orch_decision   JSONB,  -- x86 orchestration step (queue/scheduler/tokenizer)
  rdu_program_load_event   JSONB,  -- RDU bitstream loaded
  rdu_program_exec_event   JSONB,  -- RDU fabric execution
  model_weight_load_event  JSONB,  -- Samba-1 / Samba-CoE weights loaded
  kv_cache_write_event     JSONB,  -- KV cache writes (per layer)
  llm_token_emission       JSONB,  -- output tokens
  tool_call_event          JSONB,  -- tool-call to downstream agent
  agent_decision_event     JSONB,  -- multi-agent reasoning chain
  source_system_write      JSONB,  -- Snowflake / Databricks / BigQuery state change
  downstream_state_change  JSONB,  -- Postgres / S3 / Salesforce
  hardware_fw_attest       JSONB,  -- per-RDU firmware attestation
  tenant_partition_isolate JSONB,  -- per-tenant RDU partition evidence
  api_key_rotation_event   JSONB,  -- API-key rotation log
  rbac_role_change_event   JSONB,
  retention_until          TIMESTAMPTZ,
  created_at               TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW()
);

The four columns auditors actually probe — hardware_fw_attest, tenant_partition_isolate, rdu_program_load_event, model_weight_load_event — are precisely the four that most AI-chip vendors skip because they treat "we ran inference" as a black box. SambaNova's privacy@sambanova.ai inbox is the canonical GDPR DPA + SOC 2 + EU AI Act + vendor-DD strategic-inbound channel for this evidence.

5 audit gaps every AI-chip vendor will face in Q4 2026

Gap 1: Per-inference-call provenance join-table

The reference schema above is the auditor's #1 ask. Most AI-chip vendors either (a) don't log per-call events at all, (b) log only token emissions and skip hardware/program/weight/KV-cache events, or (c) log everything but don't tie it to a per-tenant RBAC + API-key-rotation join-table. The third pattern is most common and most dangerous — the data exists but cannot be defended at audit time without a 6-week back-fill.

Gap 2: Training-data transparency (Art. 53(d) GPAI)

EU AI Act Art. 53(d) requires GPAI providers to publish a "sufficiently detailed summary" of training data. For SambaNova's Samba-CoE + Samba-1 and for Groq's served Llama-3 + Mixtral + Gemma + Whisper + LLaVA, the auditor needs the upstream training-data summary + the per-model fine-tune license provenance. Both vendors must publish Apache-2.0 OSS-provenance per ISO 42001 6.1.4.

Gap 3: Prompt-injection + tool-call-manipulation detection

OWASP LLM Top 10 #1 (Prompt Injection) + #6 (Sensitive Information Disclosure) + NIST AI RMF MEASURE + EU AI Act Art. 9 (risk management) + Art. 14 (human oversight) require the AI-chip vendor to detect prompt-injection at the chip-execution layer, not just at the LLM-call layer. Detection needs to span LLM-call + tool-call + agent-decision + source-system-write + downstream-state-change + RDU/LPU-program-load-event + RDU/LPU-execution-event + model-weight-load-event + KV-cache-write-event.

Gap 4: Cross-tenant isolation-evidence

SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + HIPAA + EU AI Act Art. 10 + FedRAMP Moderate + ITAR + EAR-99 + classified-deployment patterns require per-tenant RDU/LPU-partition + per-tenant model-partition + per-tenant KV-cache-partition + per-tenant firmware/hardware-attestation + per-tenant host-CPU-orchestration-isolation. For SambaNova's US DOE / US DOD / national-lab customers, this evidence must be packaged as a per-deployment package — not as a generic "we have multi-tenancy" claim.

Gap 5: WORM retention + cost-attribution

SOC 2 CC7.2 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + SEC 17a-4 WORM + IRS 6001 + EU AI Act Annex III §4 (high-risk classification per Art. 6 + 14 + 27 + 43 + 72 + Aug 2026 GPAI enforcement) require WORM retention on the per-inference-call provenance join-table linked to per-call LLM-token-cost + per-call RDU/LPU-cycle-cost + per-call KV-cache-cost + per-call tool-call-cost + per-call agent-decision-cost + per-call source-system-write-cost + per-call downstream-state-change-cost.
