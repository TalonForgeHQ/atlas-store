1. Why this checklist exists (the OpenTelemetry-LLM audit-evidence gap nobody is filling)

Every production AI agent application deployed at a regulated enterprise today runs at least 5 audit-evidence surfaces simultaneously: (a) SOC 2 CC7.2 - operational-component system-operations monitoring criterion requiring detection of failures + response to incidents; (b) EU AI Act Art. 12 - record-keeping obligations for high-risk AI systems requiring automatic event logging over the system lifecycle; (c) EU AI Act Art. 14 - human-oversight requirements to prevent or minimize health, safety, or fundamental-rights risks; (d) ISO 42001 9.4 - AI-management-system performance evaluation + monitoring + continual improvement; (e) OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 prompt injection - the canonical 2025 OWASP LLM-application attack-surface-classification. None of these is independent; an auditor probing SOC 2 CC7.2 will pull threads into EU AI Act Art. 12 logging requirements, and an EU AI Act Annex III 4 high-risk classification triggers ISO 42001 + GDPR Art. 22 automated-decision-making + HIPAA (if PHI in LLM context) + CCPA/CPRA (if California user data) + FedRAMP Moderate (if federal AI agent deployment) all at once.

OpenLLMetry - the open-source OpenTelemetry instrumentation library for LLM applications maintained by Traceloop - sits between the application and every supported LLM provider (OpenAI + Anthropic + AWS Bedrock + Google Vertex AI + Mistral + Cohere + Hugging Face + Ollama + Groq + Together + LangChain + LlamaIndex + CrewAI + AutoGen + Haystack + Watsonx + IBM + Azure AI Foundry + Google ADK + Mistral AI + Replicate + Predibase). That makes Traceloop the natural audit-trail-instrumentation point every regulated-enterprise AI agent deployment needs. The hard truth: no OpenLLMetry-using AI agent team today produces the 11-column OTLP-trace-id-to-prompt-template-version-to-evaluation-decision-to-downstream-state-change join-table auditors will require in 2026.
