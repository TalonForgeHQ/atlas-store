Eight questions to ask before approving Respondent.io

  1. Can each participant claim be traced to verification evidence? Require a stable participant ID, verification-method ID, verification timestamp, evidence class, confidence result, expiration date, and reviewer or automated-rule version.
  2. Is screener collection proportional? Separate fields needed to establish study eligibility from fields collected only for segmentation. Record purpose, retention period, consent version, and deletion status per field.
  3. Can recruitment decisions be reconstructed? Join the study, screener, eligibility rule, participant response, exclusion reason, invitation, researcher override, and final enrollment in one exportable event trail.
  4. Are incentive terms explicit before participation? Preserve the advertised amount, currency, payment method, completion criteria, dispute path, payment status, and any platform or conversion fee shown to the participant.
  5. What prevents duplicate or synthetic participants? Ask for evidence covering duplicate-account detection, identity mismatch, copied screener answers, device or payment-account reuse, and human review without demanding invasive data by default.
  6. Are researcher and participant tenants isolated? Test screeners, messages, interview links, uploaded files, payment records, exports, support tooling, analytics, and any AI-assisted matching or summary layer.
  7. Does withdrawal propagate? A deletion request should reach profile attributes, screener responses, messages, interview artifacts, incentive records where legally permitted, derived segments, exports, backups, and downstream processors.
  8. Can a human review consequential automation? If matching, ranking, fraud screening, or eligibility uses automated logic, record the model or rule version, reason codes, appeal outcome, reviewer, and rollback timestamp.

Five failure modes to test

  • A participant is accepted as a senior buyer even though the professional-verification evidence expired before recruitment.
  • A screener collects employer, compensation, health, or demographic details that are not required for eligibility and have no field-level retention rule.
  • An automated fraud rule rejects a legitimate participant without a reason code, appeal path, or named human reviewer.
  • A completed interview is marked ineligible after the fact, but the incentive dispute cannot be reconstructed from the terms shown at enrollment.
  • A deletion request removes the participant profile while leaving screener-derived segments or researcher exports active.
