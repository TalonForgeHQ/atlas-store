36-column audit checklist

  1. per-vector-id — confirm the UUID is captured for every vector write, not a rolled-up counter.
  2. per-object-id — required to tie the vector back to the source object; aggregated counters hide the underlying record.
  3. per-collection-id — confirm multi-collection support (most vector-DB vendors only support one collection per tenant).
  4. per-tenant-id — required for multi-tenant isolation-evidence; if missing, cross-tenant-isolation cannot be proved.
  5. per-shard-id — confirm sharding is captured at the per-vector level, not aggregated.
  6. per-replica-id — required for replication-lineage; confirm each replica gets its own UUID.
  7. per-hnsw-index-id — required to identify which HNSW index served the retrieval; aggregated counts hide the actual index.
  8. per-HNSW-ef-construction — required to reconstruct the recall/quality tradeoff at retrieval time.
  9. per-HNSW-M — same rationale as ef-construction; needed for audit reconstruction.
  10. per-vector-quantization-sq8-pq-id — confirm quantization scheme (SQ8, PQ, binary) is captured per vector; affects recall.
  11. per-spfresh-vector-id — Weaviate's spfresh tiered-storage index; confirm UUID for every spfresh write.
  12. per-filter-id — confirm filter expressions are captured at the per-query level.
  13. per-filterable-metadata-field-id — required to identify which metadata field drove the filter.
  14. per-bm25-search-id — BM25 lexical-search lineage; confirm UUID for every BM25 query.
  15. per-hybrid-search-rank-id — required to reconstruct the BM25 + dense hybrid ranking at retrieval time.
  16. per-reranker-id — confirm reranker calls are captured as their own UUID.
  17. per-reranker-model-id — required to identify which reranker model (Cohere / Cross-Encoder / custom) was used.
  18. per-generation-id — every RAG generation gets a UUID; confirm tied to the originating retrieval.
  19. per-RAG-corpus-id — required to identify which corpus the retrieval drew from.
  20. per-RAG-corpus-document-id — same rationale at the document level.
  21. per-RAG-corpus-document-chunk-id — required for chunk-level lineage; this is the most-missed column in vector-DB audit-prep.
  22. per-RAG-retrieval-call-id — every retrieval is its own row, not aggregated.
  23. per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id — confirm UUID for every detection signal; this is the EU AI Act Art. 9 + Art. 14 surface.
  24. per-cross-tenant-isolation-flag — required to prove customer-A vectors never bleed into customer-B results.
  25. per-cross-tenant-KMS-CMK-BYOK — confirm customer-managed keys are enforced at the storage layer, not just at the API layer.
  26. per-data-residency-region — required for GDPR + EU AI Act + APAC data-localization; confirm per-vector capture.
  27. per-PII-redaction-tier — confirm PII redaction is enforced at the vector-DB layer, not at the RAG layer.
  28. per-customer-vector-isolation-region — required to prove customer-A vectors stay in customer-A's region.
  29. per-zero-retention-flag — Weaviate zero-retention is on by default for Enterprise; confirm.
  30. per-customer-tenant-id — required to tie every column above to a specific tenant.
  31. per-self-hosted-instance-id — required to identify which self-hosted instance served the query.
  32. per-on-prem-instance-flag — required to confirm on-prem deployments are flagged separately.
  33. per-VPC-peering-id — required to identify which VPC peering served the query.
  34. per-air-gapped-deployment-flag — Weaviate air-gapped is supported on Enterprise; confirm the flag is actually set.
  35. per-SSO-SAML-OIDC-config-id — required to tie identity to retrieval events.
  36. per-SCIM-provisioning-id — required for user-lifecycle audit-trail.
  37. per-audit-log-export-id — confirm export to S3 / Splunk / Datadog is configured and live.
