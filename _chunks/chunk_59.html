Gap 3 — Cross-tenant eval-platform + simulation-backend isolation evidence

Frameworks: SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + HIPAA + FedRAMP Moderate + EU AI Act Art. 10

For the multi-tenant Patronus Enterprise Eval Platform running eval-decisions on the Datadog + Samsung + Fortune-500 enterprise customer stack, the auditor will specifically probe judge-LLM isolation across tenants because the judge LLM is the natural cross-tenant-data-leakage surface if isolation isn't per-tenant-per-judge-call. A judge's response could leak sensitive eval-prompt material (which may contain customer-confidential agent-design-IP) into another tenant's downstream eval context. The evidence package:

  • Per-tenant isolation-test-result: pen-test report showing a tenant-A eval_run cannot read tenant-B eval_run judge-decisions (read or write) — including the judge-LLM intermediate reasoning tokens.
  • Per-tenant CMK/BYOK optionality: can the customer bring their own KMS key for at-rest encryption of eval-run artifacts + judge-decision logs + Digital World Model sampling logs?
  • Per-eval-run-no-leakage-evidence: per-eval-run isolation test result.
  • Per-tenant world-data-artifact-residue-cleanup-procedure: when a tenant offboards, is the per-tenant sampled-artifact-residue purged from the customer's downstream artifacts + the shared Digital World Model sampling log + the cache + the log indexes within SLA?
  • Completion-of-deletion timestamp: signed attestation of completion-of-deletion per tenant.
  • Per-customer-no-leakage-evidence: per-tenant attestation that no eval-run artifacts leaked across the tenant boundary during the reporting period.
