3-step vendor-DD playbook

  1. Step 1 — Map your footprint. Are you a regulated banking customer needing BankAssist-grade vertical depth? If yes, Kore.ai wins the bake-off by default — Moveworks doesn't ship a banking-vertical product. If you're a regulated federal contractor needing FedRAMP Moderate AI workloads, Moveworks wins by default — Kore.ai is FedRAMP-ready but not yet authorized. If neither, you have a real bake-off and the remaining 7 axes decide.
  2. Step 2 — 3-year TCO. Kore.ai at 1M tickets/yr is ~$1.40/ticket × 1M = $1.4M/yr. Moveworks at 1M tickets/yr is ~$1.20/ticket × 1M = $1.2M/yr. The 17% delta is roughly $600K over 3 years — material but not the deciding factor. The deciding factor is the EU AI Act Aug 2026 readiness gap: Kore.ai's ISO 27701 + EU AI Act ready posture closes the audit-trail gap that Moveworks's ISO 27701 + FedRAMP Moderate posture doesn't directly cover for EU customers.
  3. Step 3 — 90-day pilot. Run both Kore.ai and Moveworks on the same 5-ticket basket: (a) password reset for a blocked role, (b) credit-card dispute intake, (c) health-insurance enrollment change, (d) cross-border vendor onboarding, (e) AI-agent audit-trail export. The vendor that ships the per-conversational-AI-decision audit trail Table Stakes (16-column join-table below) wins the 90-day pilot. The vendor that doesn't loses the procurement cycle.
