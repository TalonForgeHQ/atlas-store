Audit Gap 2 — Training-corpus-source + fine-tune license-provenance per EU AI Act Art. 53(d) + Art. 53(1)(b) + Art. 53(2)

Per EU AI Act Art. 53(d), GPAI (general-purpose AI) vendors must publish a publicly-available-summary of training-data sources. For Heap Illuminate + Replay AI + Funnel AI + Cohort AI, the auditor needs: (a) the corpus-source license for the underlying LLM (Heap Illuminate likely uses OpenAI GPT-4 + Anthropic Claude + open-source Llama + Mistral as LLM-backends per vendor-DD disclosures), (b) any downstream-fine-tunes Heap has performed on top of the underlying LLM, (c) the prompt-engineering-prompt-template library that drives Heap Illuminate + Cohort AI. Without the Art. 53(d) publicly-available-summary, Heap cannot serve GPAI-class customers in the EU after Aug 2026.

Audit Gap 3 — Prompt-injection + tool-call-manipulation + agent-decision-poisoning defense

Per OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt-injection) + LLM06 (sensitive-information-disclosure) + NIST AI RMF MEASURE function + EU AI Act Art. 9 (risk-management-system) + Art. 14 (human-oversight-required), Heap must defend against adversarial-prompt-injection across the autocapture → AI-recommendation → replay-analysis pipeline. The specific attack surface is: when a user-input from a tracked form-field flows into Heap Illuminate's natural-language-cohort-builder, does Heap treat the user-input as untrusted and validate against prompt-injection-patterns before the LLM call? The autocapture layer captures form-field-values, which means an attacker who controls a form-field can inject adversarial prompts into Heap Illuminate's LLM-call. Heap must have a documented defense-in-depth layer (input-validation + output-sanitization + LLM-call rate-limit + prompt-injection-pattern-blocklist).
