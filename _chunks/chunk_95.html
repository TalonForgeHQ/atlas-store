5-layer reference architecture

L1 — Provenance capture

Every Honeycomb event (span + trace + log + AI-call-record) writes to a WORM-locked audit-table at the ingestion-edge. The provenance layer captures per-event-uuid + per-tenant-id + per-trace-id + per-AI-call-uuid + per-prompt-text + per-LLM-token-cost + per-AI-decision-id + per-tool-call-id + per-downstream-state-change-id + per-BubbleUp-correlated-dimension-id + per-AI-disclosure-label. The high-cardinality wide-events store preserves every dimension on every event, so the per-AI-decision reasoning-chain is recoverable from the raw event-stream with no aggregation loss.

L2 — WORM cost-attribution

The 6-column cost-attribution join-table (per-AI-Query-Assistant-token-cost + per-BubbleUp-correlation-cost + per-Trigger-cost + per-Service-Map-cost + per-pipeline-refinery-cost + per-downstream-state-change-cost) is WORM-locked for the 7-year retention window per SEC 17a-4 + IRS 6001. The join-table is the cost-driver that finance + procurement teams use to attribute AI-driven costs back to specific tenants + events + decisions. The join-table is also the WORM-locked ledger that auditors use to verify that no AI-call has been silently dropped + no AI-decision has been retroactively altered.

L3 — Cross-tenant isolation-evidence

The isolation-evidence document covers per-tenant high-cardinality-event-data + per-tenant AI-Query-isolation + per-tenant BubbleUp-isolation + per-tenant Trigger-isolation for the Honeycomb SaaS + Enterprise multi-tenant infrastructure. The document is refreshed quarterly to capture any infrastructure changes from the Honeycomb Telemetry Pipeline + Refinery + Triggers + Service Map surface. The cross-tenant isolation-evidence is the auditor's reverse-trace document for SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + HIPAA + EU AI Act Art. 10 + FedRAMP Moderate.

L4 — AI-pipeline transparency

Every Honeycomb AI Query Assistant call + every BubbleUp correlation + every Trigger decision + every Service Map fingerprint is tagged with ai_disclosure_label per EU AI Act Art. 50 (transparency-obligation for AI-system-output). The ai_disclosure_label surfaces in the Honeycomb dashboard + every customer-facing report + every AI-feature-flagged anomaly-result. Every AI-result that drives a downstream-state-change is tagged with downstream_agent_decision_ids linkage for the auditor's reverse-join from the downstream-state-change back to the Honeycomb AI-call that drove the change.

L5 — Compliance substrate

The compliance substrate covers SOC 2 CC6.1 + CC6.7 + CC7.2 + ISO 42001 6.1.4 + 9.4 + ISO 27001 + GDPR Art. 26 + Art. 28 + HIPAA §164.316(b)(2)(i) + SEC 17a-4 WORM + IRS 6001 + EU AI Act Art. 9 + Art. 10 + Art. 12 + Art. 14 + Art. 50 + Art. 53(d) + Art. 53(1)(b) + Art. 53(2) + Annex III §4 + Aug 2026 GPAI enforcement. The substrate is the auditor's reading-list that proves Honeycomb's compliance posture for the 7-product-line AI surface.
