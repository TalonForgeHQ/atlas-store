AI Agent Audit Trail for Fortune 500 Document AI: What Your GC Asks the Day After a Mistake

If your engineering team is shipping an AI agent that summarizes 10-Ks, red-lines IDDs, extracts tables from S-1s, or answers "give me every covenant breach in this credit agreement" from inside a customer's per-tenant data room, your general counsel will eventually ask a question no demo deck prepares you for: "When the LLM told our deal team the wrong EBITDA, can you show me exactly which tokens, which page, which model version, and which prompt produced that number — and can you hand it to the SEC if they ask tomorrow?" That question is the audit trail. The companies that can answer it in under 30 minutes win the Fortune 500 deal cycle. The companies that can't, lose the renewal 14 months later. This chunk walks through what a buyer-ready AI agent audit trail looks like for document-AI products specifically — different from chat-tool-call trails because the surface is multi-million-token context windows, not 50-message threads.

The 4 evidence surfaces a Fortune 500 buyer expects to see

  1. Per-page source attribution with immutable provenance: For every number, paragraph, or table the agent surfaces, your system should store a tuple of (document_id, page_number, paragraph_offset, bounding_box_hash, model_version, prompt_template_id, completion_id, retrieved_chunk_ids[], confidence_score). Hebbia, Glean, and Harvey all surface this in some form for their enterprise tier — the buyers will compare your attribution fidelity to theirs on the security questionnaire before the demo even gets scheduled.
  2. Per-document-grounded LLM completion log: A WORM-locked log (S3 Object Lock in compliance mode, or equivalent) of every completion the agent produced, with the full prompt + full completion text + every retrieved chunk's embedding + every cited source's hash. SEC 17a-4 + IRS 6001 retention rules mean WORM is non-negotiable if the AI is making statements that flow into filings. Buyers know this; if your security page doesn't say "WORM 7-year," they assume you don't have it.
  3. Per-customer tenant isolation with verifiable data-residency: If you're selling to a European bank, the audit will ask where the vector embeddings live, where the LLM calls route, whether the retrieval index is shared, and whether your evaluation harness has ever sampled another tenant's data into this customer's evaluation set. SOC 2 CC6.1 + EU AI Act Art. 10 + GDPR Art. 28 are all in scope; the audit trail you ship needs to answer each one with a row of evidence, not a paragraph of policy.
  4. Per-LLM-call cost and carbon attribution joined to the document: Fortune 500 procurement now requires cost attribution per business unit, per deal team, per matter, per project — and per-document. If your agent can tell a customer "the Senior Associate's cost on this Project Atlas was $14.32 of LLM spend across 1.4M tokens, and the CO2e was 380 grams," you will win the renewal. If you can't, the customer's finance team will build a parallel cost ledger in Excel and your platform becomes the canvas, not the source of truth.

What auditors specifically call out for document-AI agents

AICPA SOC 2 2026 Trust Services Criteria explicitly added "training data governance" and "automated decision provenance" as in-scope Common Criteria. For a document-AI agent, that maps to four discrete evidence rows: (a) which version of which model produced the completion (and can you demonstrate model downgrade procedures if a regulator asks for "the model that was live on March 4"), (b) what retrieval index version the agent queried, (c) what prompt template version was active, and (d) what evaluation suite result was logged for the agent in the 30 days surrounding the completion. Buyers running SOC 2 Type II will pull a sample of 25-40 document-AI completions across the audit window. If any of them have an ambiguous model version or a missing retrieval-index version, your Type II report carries a finding that propagates into every Fortune 500 customer's vendor-DD refresh for the next 12 months.

The 7-row evidence template that closes the gap in 30 days

The fastest way to ship this if you have an existing document-AI product in market is to add seven rows to your existing llm_completions table: source_document_hash, source_page_offset, retrieval_index_version, model_version_id, prompt_template_id, evaluation_suite_run_id, citation_provenance_hash. Each is a string or a UUID; each is null-tolerant for legacy rows. Backfilling 90 days of history takes less than a week. The audit window opens quarterly; the buyer-side checklist above is the gap analysis your security team runs before the Type II fieldwork starts.
