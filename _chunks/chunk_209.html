Why this spec exists (and why no LLM-observability vendor ships it fully yet)

Every LLM-observability vendor in the market — Helicone, Langfuse, Arize AI, Galileo AI, Datadog LLM Observability, New Relic AI Monitoring, Splunk-Cisco Observability — collects per-LLM-call-id telemetry. Each stores it slightly differently. Each gives you a different slice of the join-table needed to reconstruct what actually happened in production.

The problem shows up the first time a regulated-industry customer (a bank, a hospital, an insurance carrier, a federal agency) runs into one of these audit questions:

  • "A customer filed a complaint about an LLM-generated claim denial on March 4. Show me the exact prompt-template-version-commit-id that was active when the call was made, and prove the LLM call (per-LLM-call-id) routed through that prompt-version and not v6."
  • "Our EU regulator wants proof that the prompt-template we A/B tested against a high-risk customer cohort did not produce biased outputs. Reconstruct every per-LLM-call-id in cohort B and join it to per-eval-result-id + per-LLM-call-cost-usd + per-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id."
  • "An auditor is asking us to demonstrate that our RAG retrieval pipeline didn't surface poisoned documents into a regulated decision. Show me per-RAG-corpus-document-chunk-id + per-RAG-retrieval-event-id + per-RAG-rerank-event-id for every LLM call that touched customer PII."
  • "A SOC 2 CC7.2 auditor wants to confirm our cache hit rate didn't bypass safety filters. Prove that every per-cache-hit-miss-flag is joined to per-cache-similarity-threshold-id + per-cache-key-id + per-LLM-call-prompt-injection-detection-signal-id so we can demonstrate semantic-equivalence."
  • "We had a hallucination incident in our patient-facing chatbot. Reconstruct every per-LLM-call-id that went into the response, including the per-tool-call-id + per-tool-call-result-id chain and the per-eval-result-id that should have caught it."

None of the seven LLM-observability vendors above ships all 60+ columns of this join-table out of the box. Most ship 15-25 of them. Some don't even expose the per-prompt-template-version-id and per-prompt-template-commit-id as separate fields — they conflate them. Others don't expose per-cache-similarity-threshold-id at all, so cache-hit provenance is unrecoverable.

The 60+ column join-table (the actual spec)

The table below is the minimum viable schema for "production-LLM audit-target reconstruction" that survives vendor-DD + SOC 2 + GDPR Art. 30 + EU AI Act Art. 12 + ISO 42001 + OWASP LLM Top 10. It is organized into 8 logical groups:

Group 1: Per-LLM-call identity (10 columns)

per-LLM-call-id (UUID, primary key) | per-org-id | per-project-id | per-environment-id (dev/staging/prod) | per-deployment-id | per-region-id | per-LLM-call-timestamp-utc | per-LLM-call-latency-ms | per-LLM-call-model-provider-id (OpenAI/Anthropic/Bedrock/Vertex/Azure/Mistral/Groq/Together/Replicate/Fireworks/Cohere/Local) | per-model-id (gpt-4o / claude-opus-4 / llama-3.1-70b / etc.)

Group 2: Per-prompt-template provenance (8 columns)

per-prompt-template-id | per-prompt-template-version-id | per-prompt-template-commit-id (git SHA) | per-prompt-environment-id (dev/staging/prod prompt registry) | per-prompt-A-B-test-id | per-feature-flag-id | per-prompt-deployment-id | per-prompt-template-author-id

Group 3: Per-completion + token economics (7 columns)

per-completion-id | per-LLM-call-tokens-input | per-LLM-call-tokens-output | per-LLM-call-tokens-total | per-LLM-call-cost-usd | per-LLM-call-cache-hit-flag | per-LLM-call-cache-savings-usd

Group 4: Per-stream + tool-call chain (5 columns)

per-LLM-call-stream-flag | per-LLM-call-function-call-flag | per-LLM-call-tool-call-flag | per-tool-call-id | per-tool-call-result-id

Group 5: Per-RAG retrieval chain (4 columns)

per-RAG-corpus-document-chunk-id | per-RAG-retrieval-event-id | per-RAG-rerank-event-id | per-RAG-embedding-id
