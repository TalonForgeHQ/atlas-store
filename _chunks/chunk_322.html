Five audit gaps to test before approving AlphaSense + Tegus

1. Citation-to-source reconstruction

Pick a sample of AI-generated answers and reconstruct each statement to the exact filing page, earnings-call timestamp, research note, event record, or expert-call segment. Record missing citations, stale index versions, inaccessible licensed sources, and any answer whose evidence changed after generation.

2. Expert-call MNPI and wall-crossing controls

Test whether Tegus expert-call content carries expert identity controls, consent and license provenance, call date, restricted-list checks, MNPI flags, wall-crossing status, review disposition, and downstream citation lineage. Map the control to FINRA Rule 5280, SEC Rule 10b-5 policies, and the buyer’s insider-trading procedures.

3. Licensed research and training-data provenance

Separate rights to display or summarize broker research from rights to use that material for retrieval, evaluation, tuning, or model training. Maintain source-level license terms, territory, retention period, deletion obligations, and model-use restrictions for every corpus class.

4. Cross-tenant and cross-firm isolation

Prove that queries, watchlists, uploaded documents, expert-call access, prompts, summaries, and audit logs cannot cross AlphaSense tenants or customer firms. Capture tenant key identifiers, CMK/BYOK configuration where available, region, role, policy decision, and negative isolation-test evidence.

5. WORM retention and cost attribution

Retain the query-to-answer reconstruction package for the buyer’s required period and test export into a WORM-capable archive. Add per-query, per-document, per-summary, per-expert-call, per-workflow, and per-business-unit cost fields so finance can reconcile usage without weakening evidence retention.
