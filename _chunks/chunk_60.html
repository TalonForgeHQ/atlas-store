The audit surface is fundamentally different from every other framework in the ai_agents_infra cluster:

Gap 4 — Cross-tenant AutoGen Studio + Magentic-One-Cloud isolation-evidence

Frameworks: SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + HIPAA + FedRAMP Moderate + EU AI Act Art. 10

For the multi-tenant SaaS deployment (Goldman Sachs + Bloomberg + BCG + Deloitte + KPMG + IBM enterprise customers), the audit surface is a per-tenant isolation-evidence package:

  • Per-tenant isolation-test-result — proof that tenant-A's GroupChat thread + Magentic-One Orchestrator plan + code-execution sandbox cannot leak into tenant-B's execution context.
  • Per-tenant CMK/BYOK optionality — proof that each tenant can supply their own customer-managed-keys for at-rest encryption of thread + plan + sandbox telemetry.
  • Per-conversation-thread-no-leakage-evidence — proof that tenant-A's GroupChat thread payload + cross-agent-context-window content cannot be read by tenant-B's agents or sandbox.
  • Per-tenant fine-tune-residue-cleanup-procedure — proof that when a tenant leaves, any Microsoft-internal fine-tune-residue from that tenant's Magentic-One deployment is cleaned up.
  • Completion-of-deletion timestamp — per GDPR Art. 17 right-to-erasure + CCPA + FedRAMP Moderate SAR.
  • Per-customer-no-leakage-evidence — quarterly penetration-test report scoped to the multi-tenant AutoGen Studio + Magentic-One-Cloud boundary.

The auditor will specifically probe Magentic-One isolation because the browser + file-surfer + coder + executor agents are the natural cross-tenant-data-leakage surface if isolation is not per-tenant-per-agent-call — and the coder agent's filesystem-write capability makes this the audit lane to harden before the next FedRAMP Moderate review.
