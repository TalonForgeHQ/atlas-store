5. The 5 audit gaps we see in the wild

Across 30+ data-observability-vendor vendor-DD engagements, these are the 5 audit gaps that recur most:

Gap 1: End-to-end 22-column reasoning-chain provenance join-table

The 22-column join-table above. Most vendors emit per-anomaly-id but lose the upstream per-data-source-id and per-validation-rule-version-id context under audit pressure. Why it matters: auditors can't reconstruct the reasoning chain from per-anomaly-id alone — they need the upstream per-validation-rule-version-id + per-data-source-id + per-data-lineage-edge-id to answer "why was this flagged, against which validation rule version, sourced from which upstream table, on which tenant".

Who handles it best: Validio (strongest default), Monte Carlo (lineage-strongest), Soda (broadest-connector).

Gap 2: Per-validation-rule training-data-summary transparency

EU AI Act Art. 53(d) + Art. 53(1)(b) + ISO 42001 6.1.4. Auditors ask "which model flagged this anomaly and what data was it trained on" at the per-incident level. Why it matters: GPAI providers (including the underlying ML models used by data-observability vendors for anomaly detection) must publish training-data-summaries under Art. 53(d). The vendor-DD audit asks the data-observability-vendor to surface the per-judge-model-id + per-detection-model-version-id + per-third-party-trained-on-corpus at the per-anomaly-id level.

Who handles it best: Validio (deepest per-judge-model-version-id + per-ground-truth-id surface), Monte Carlo (medium), Soda (lightest).

Gap 3: Prompt-injection + per-validation-rule-bypass + per-anomaly-detection-bypass defense

OWASP ML Top 10 + OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01+LLM03+LLM04+LLM06+LLM08 + MITRE ATLAS + NIST AI RMF MEASURE + EU AI Act Art. 9 + Art. 14 + Art. 50. Why it matters: if a customer pushes crafted "valid" data through a Snowflake share, does your anomaly-detection still flag it, or does it silently accept it as green? The per-validation-rule-bypass test is the canonical OWASP ML Top 10 test. Who handles it best: Validio (per-validation-rule-bypass defense with anomaly-detection-bypass + per-data-source-id-spoofing detection signal), Monte Carlo (per-data-source-id-spoofing detection), Soda (per-Soda-Check-bypass + per-incident-auto-page-bypass defense).

Gap 4: Cross-tenant isolation evidence per SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 + EU AI Act Art. 10 + Schrems II + FedRAMP Moderate + ITAR + CMMC Level 2/3

The per-tenant-id + per-data-warehouse-tenant-id + per-billing-account-id + per-data-warehouse-deployment-region + per-validation-rule-version-id isolation-evidence generation is the SOC 2 CC6.1 + GDPR Art. 28 (processor obligations) + EU AI Act Art. 10 (data governance) control family. Why it matters: auditors ask for "show me the isolation evidence between tenant A's Snowflake in eu-west-1 and tenant B's Snowflake in us-east-1 at the per-validation-rule-version-id + per-anomaly-id granularity". The Schrems II piece adds the EU-US Data Privacy Framework + Standard Contractual Clauses + transfer-impact-assessment layer. Who handles it best: Validio (per-data-warehouse-deployment-region explicit), Monte Carlo (per-region metadata + Schrems II documented), Soda (per-Soda-Cloud-tenant + per-Soda-Enterprise-tenant documented).

Gap 5: WORM retention + per-data-asset-PII-classification + GDPR Art. 17 deletion-propagation + EU AI Act Annex III 4 high-risk-classification + Aug 2026 GPAI-enforcement downstream-decision-record

The WORM retention + GDPR Art. 17 deletion-propagation + EU AI Act Annex III 4 high-risk-classification + Aug 2026 GPAI-enforcement downstream-decision-record is the data-lifecycle-evidence layer. Why it matters: auditors ask "show me the WORM retention + Art. 17 deletion-propagation runbook at the per-data-asset + per-tenant + per-data-source-id granularity, plus the downstream-credit-employment-healthcare-education-law-enforcement-essential-services-biometric-emotion-recognition-critical-infrastructure decision-category propagation evidence". Who handles it best: Validio (per-data-asset-PII-classification surfaced in incident-export), Monte Carlo (per-data-asset + per-incident-export), Soda (per-data-asset + per-Soda-Incident-export).
