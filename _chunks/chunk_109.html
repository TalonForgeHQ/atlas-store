Anyscale is the canonical Ray-on-Anyscale distributed-compute vendor behind Anyscale Endpoints (LLM inference per-second billing), Anyscale Jobs (managed Ray batch), Anyscale Workspaces (interactive dev environments) and the Anyscale Enterprise control plane. They are also the corporate steward of the open-source Ray project (Apache 2.0, 35K+ GitHub stars, co-authored by Ion Stoica, Robert Nishihara and Philipp Moritz at UC Berkeley's RISELab). For a privacy officer or security architect under EU AI Act, ISO 42001 or NIS2 timelines, Anyscale is structurally interesting because the privacy surface inherits Ray's distributed cluster model: per-customer isolated cluster, per-job ephemeral execution, per-token billing metadata, and per-call PII handling that maps to GDPR Art. 22 (automated decision-making), Art. 14 (information about processing) and Annex III (high-risk biometric / critical infrastructure) classifications.

What "Ray-Anyscale" actually is

The Ray project started in UC Berkeley's RISELab around 2017 as a unified interface for distributed Python (data preprocessing, hyperparameter sweeps, RL training, batch inference, model serving). Anyscale, the company, was founded in 2019 by Ion Stoica (UC Berkeley Professor + Databricks co-founder + Apache Spark PMC), Robert Nishihara (Anyscale CEO + Ray creator) and Philipp Moritz (Ray co-creator + Anyscale Co-Founder) to commercialize Ray's managed runtime. Anyscale Endpoints exposes open-source LLMs (Llama-3, Mixtral, DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM, Gemma, Phi, Stable-Diffusion) as an OpenAI-compatible inference API with per-second GPU billing, function-calling, fine-tuning, and RLHF pipelines. Anyscale Jobs gives you a serverless-Ray-batch-engine for ad-hoc jobs (image preprocessing at scale, embedding generation, batch inference). Anyscale Workspaces gives you a managed JupyterLab-tier dev environment running inside the customer's Anyscale cluster.

For an enterprise customer evaluating vendor-DD on Anyscale, the architecture question is "how does my code, my weights and my data stay isolated from other tenants?" Anyscale's documented answers are: (1) per-customer Anyscale Enterprise cluster with dedicated VPC + encryption-at-rest (AES-256) + encryption-in-transit (TLS 1.2+, mTLS for cluster-internal RPC); (2) per-job ephemeral execution with no cross-tenant inference traffic; (3) per-call PII handling flagged for downstream-Salesforce / HubSpot / Databricks / Snowflake / BigQuery state-changes; (4) SOC 2 Type II (per anyscale.com/security), GDPR DPA, EU AI Act readiness, ISO 27001 in progress.

Anyscale vendor-DD playbook (Talon Forge Atlas, 8-step)

  1. Locate the privacy-policy surface. Canonical anchor: anyscale.com/privacy-policy. The page exposes mailto:privacy@anyscale.com as the canonical GDPR DPA + SOC 2 + EU AI Act + vendor-DD strategic-inbound channel. Verified live via direct HTTP fetch on 2026-07-12.
  2. Initiate vendor-DD outreach via the privacy channel — not sales. Anyscale's privacy@ distribution is monitored by both the legal team (privacy@ for GDPR DPA, EU AI Act, ISO 42001) and the security team (security@ for SOC 2 attestation, ISO 27001 status, pen-test summary). The vendor-DD approach via privacy@ produces faster, more substantive responses than cold-outreach to AEs.
  3. Ask for the EU AI Act readiness packet. The packet includes: Article 27 EU representative identity, Article 14 information-notice template, Annex III risk classification, ISO 42001 audit-status; expected turnaround 5-10 business days.
  4. Validate SOC 2 Type II scope — confirm the audit window covers the 12 months preceding your contract start, and that the audit scope includes all of Anyscale Endpoints + Anyscale Jobs + Anyscale Workspaces (not just the management plane).
  5. Validate ISO 27001 status — confirm whether the certificate is in progress (Anyscale is mid-cert) or already awarded; if mid-cert, capture the target cert date.
  6. Validate GDPR DPA + Annex III + Art. 22 + Art. 14 — request the signed DPA, the Article 27 EU representative identity (Anyscale's EU rep is appointed), the per-call PII-handling notice, and the per-job-ephemeral-execution guarantee.
  7. Validate data-residency posture — confirm US vs EU vs multi-region cluster availability, per-customer VPC isolation, per-job ephemeral-storage teardown, per-customer-weights-at-rest encryption posture.
  8. Validate billing + metering transparency — Anyscale's per-second GPU-billing is one of the few inference-platform vendors with transparent metering; confirm you can export per-job billing feeds into your downstream-Snowflake / BigQuery / Databricks cost-allocation pipeline.
