Seven questions to ask before approving an AI research repository

  1. Can every recording be joined to participant consent? Require participant_id, consent_event_id, research_study_id, recording_id, purpose_id, and customer_tenant_id in one exportable row.
  2. Is sensitive data removed before model access? Distinguish capture-time masking, transcription-time redaction, prompt-time filtering, and human overrides. Record the redaction-policy version and exception decision for every segment sent to a model.
  3. Can every AI note or repository answer cite its source? Join ai_note_id, theme_id, and repository_answer_id to the exact transcript segment, recording timestamp, model version, prompt version, retrieval query, and cited clip.
  4. What blocks prompt injection through participant speech? Interview transcripts and uploaded notes are untrusted content. Require input delimiting, tool restrictions, retrieval filtering, output validation, and human approval under OWASP LLM01 and LLM06.
  5. Are customers isolated throughout search and synthesis? Check recordings, transcripts, vector indexes, caches, evaluation datasets, exports, model gateways, and support tooling against SOC 2 CC6.1 and GDPR Art. 28.
  6. Does deletion propagate everywhere? A participant deletion must reach the recording, transcript, clip, note, embedding, AI theme, repository answer, evaluation copy, export, and backup-expiry record.
  7. Who approved a research conclusion? Link every report export, product recommendation, Jira or Linear write, and stakeholder summary to human_approval_id, purpose, timestamp, and rollback record.

The minimum interview-to-insight provenance row

A procurement-ready export should contain: participant_id, consent_event_id, research_study_id, recording_id, transcript_segment_id, redaction_policy_version_id, ai_note_id, theme_id, repository_answer_id, model_version_id, prompt_version_id, retrieval_query_id, cited_clip_id, human_approval_id, customer_tenant_id, residency_region_id, deletion_request_id, and audit_log_entry_id.

This join supports SOC 2 CC7.2 event reconstruction, EU AI Act Art. 12 record keeping, Art. 14 human oversight and Art. 50 transparency, GDPR Arts. 5, 17 and 28, and ISO 42001 evidence evaluation.

Five failure modes buyers should test

  • A participant withdraws consent, but a transcript fragment remains in a vector index or evaluation dataset.
  • A health detail, customer name, email address, or confidential roadmap item reaches the model before redaction.
  • An adversarial sentence in a transcript changes the AI summary or causes retrieval of another study.
  • A repository answer presents a theme without source segments, timestamps, or contrary evidence.
  • A generated recommendation reaches a downstream workflow without a named reviewer or approval timestamp.
