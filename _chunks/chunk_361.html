Why Linear is the high-leverage AI work-management audit target

Gap 1 - Source-issue-to-agent-action-to-downstream-sync provenance

A Linear Agent, Ask Linear query, AI Triage decision, AI Project Update draft, or AI Filter/Sort/Assign/Estimate/Draft/Resolve mutation can draw context from issues, projects, cycles, initiatives, labels, customer requests, comments, attachments, Slack threads, Notion pages, Loom recordings, GitHub PRs, and connected Google or Microsoft 365 data. A defensible evidence chain should join the source-object ID and version to retrieved chunks, prompt and model versions, agent plan, tool calls, approvals, the resulting mutation, and every downstream sync to Slack, Notion, GitHub, Loom, Figma, and customer-facing summaries.

Audit question: for one issue whose assignee, priority, label, project, cycle, estimate, comment, sub-issue, parent, or resolved state changed because of an AI action, can an administrator reconstruct the exact source objects, retrieved context, prompt, model, plan, tool call, actor, approval, timestamp, before/after state, and the downstream sync log entries that flowed to Slack, Notion, GitHub, Loom, Figma, and any customer notification?

Gap 2 - Prompt-injection defense across issue content and connected apps

Issue content is untrusted input. A malicious instruction can arrive in an issue title, description, comment, sub-issue, attachment, GitHub PR body, Slack thread, Notion page, Loom transcript, customer email, customer-request body, or connected search result. A control map should show how Linear separates content from instructions, constrains tools, detects indirect injection, redacts or escapes risky embedded payloads, and records blocked or escalated attempts.

Audit question: what prevents an embedded instruction in a retrieved issue description, attachment, PR diff, Slack thread, Notion page, or customer email from causing an Ask Linear answer, AI Triage decision, AI Project Update, or AI Filter/Sort/Assign/Estimate/Draft/Resolve mutation that discloses data, escalates the wrong ticket, posts into a customer-facing channel, triggers an automation, or writes into a connected system?

Gap 3 - Workspace, team, label, view, customer, and connector isolation

Linear’s hierarchy includes workspace, team, project, cycle, label, view, initiative, customer, customer request, document, user, and guest scopes, plus external connectors and regional residency. Agent authorization should be evaluated at every retrieval and action boundary rather than inherited once at session start.

Audit question: does each Ask Linear retrieval, AI Triage classification, AI Project Update draft, and AI Filter/Sort/Assign/Estimate/Draft/Resolve mutation re-check the initiating principal, current object permissions, guest restrictions, customer restrictions, connector scopes, data-residency policy, and destination authorization before reading or mutating data, syncing to Slack/Notion/GitHub/Loom, or surfacing content in a view that crosses team or customer boundaries?

Gap 4 - Deletion, retention, rollback, and downstream-sync propagation

An agent-originated change may create derived summaries, sub-issues, comments, notifications, customer updates, project updates, search-index entries, exports, and downstream writes to Slack, Notion, GitHub, Loom, Figma, and connected Google or Microsoft 365 surfaces. Deleting the source object or rolling back the action should propagate predictably across every derived copy and connector without silently restoring stale data later.

Audit question: when an administrator deletes or rolls back one AI-resolved, AI-estimated, AI-assigned, AI-prioritized, AI-drafted, or AI-summarized Linear action, what evidence proves propagation through Linear issue history, sub-issues, comments, project updates, customer requests, views, search index, and bounded backups, plus the downstream Slack/Notion/GitHub/Loom/Figma/Google/Microsoft sync surfaces that received the change?

Gap 5 - Immutable human-approval evidence and per-agent cost attribution

High-impact actions should preserve an immutable approval record containing the proposed action, risk classification, approver, policy version, final payload, and execution outcome. The same chain should attribute model, retrieval, storage, connector, and tool-call costs to the responsible agent run, workspace, team, project, and customer so finance can reconcile AI spend.

Audit question: can Linear produce a tamper-evident approval and execution record for AI-resolved, AI-estimated, AI-assigned, AI-drafted, and AI-summarized actions, then reconcile its cost ledger from agent run to model call, retrieved context, tool call, mutation, downstream sync, and customer?
