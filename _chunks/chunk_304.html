Seven questions to ask before approving a synthetic-media vendor

  1. Is every voice clone joined to a consent record? Require voice_clone_id, consent_event_id, consent_method (TCPA prior-express-written-consent / BIPA biometric-release / GDPR Art. 9 explicit-consent / Illinois AI Video Interview Act written-notice), consent_withdrawal_event_id, and tenant_id in one exportable row.
  2. Is every render joined to script + translation + avatar + voice? Distinguish per-render render_job_id, avatar_id, voice_clone_id, ai_script_id, ai_translation_id, model_version_id, prompt_version_id, and tenant_id in the same row, with a render_timestamp and residency_region_id.
  3. Can the platform defend against deepfake + voice-clone-misuse? Require per-render provenance metadata (C2PA Content Credentials or equivalent), watermark, and a named reviewer for any render above a configurable trust threshold. Tie the defense to OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01/LLM06 and EU AI Act Art. 14 human oversight.
  4. Does the AI script inherit disclosure obligations? If a Synthesia video reaches audiences in California, Colorado, Illinois, Texas, New York, or 7 other states with AI-disclosure statutes, the script and render must carry a ai_disclosure_state_set field plus a per-state notice template.
  5. Are tenants isolated through the full render path? Check avatar instances, voice instances, script storage, translation caches, model gateways, render queues, and watermarking keys — not only the primary database — against SOC 2 CC6.1, GDPR Art. 28, and Schrems II.
  6. Can the vendor honor voice-clone consent withdrawal? A consent-withdrawal event must propagate to the voice_clone_id (no new renders), to existing renders (clear tombstone + flag), to caches (purge), to evaluation datasets (purge), and to backup-expiry records. Preserve an auditable tombstone without retaining the cloned voice biometric.
  7. Who approved the render for the intended audience? Link each published video to human_approval_id, approver_role, intended_audience, intended_jurisdiction, and rollback_record_id for SOC 2 CC7.2, EU AI Act Art. 14, and 12-state AI-disclosure evidence.

The minimum provenance row

A procurement-ready evidence export should contain at least: render_job_id, avatar_id, voice_clone_id, consent_event_id, consent_method, consent_withdrawal_event_id, ai_script_id, ai_translation_id, model_version_id, prompt_version_id, c2pa_provenance_id, ai_disclosure_state_set, human_approval_id, intended_audience, customer_tenant_id, residency_region_id, and audit_log_entry_id.

This single join supports SOC 2 CC7.2 event reconstruction, EU AI Act Art. 12 record keeping and Art. 14 human oversight and Art. 50 transparency, GDPR Arts. 5, 9, and 28, FCC TCPA prior-express-written-consent, Illinois BIPA biometric-privacy consent, Illinois AI Video Interview Act written-notice, and 12-state AI-disclosure.

Five failure modes buyers should test

  • A voice clone persists after consent withdrawal, with new renders queued.
  • A render to a California or Colorado audience ships without the required AI-presenter disclosure.
  • An AI script contains a prompt-injection attempt that alters translation or suppresses compliance text in the translated output.
  • A healthcare-tenant render shares a render queue with a financial-services tenant, leaking draft script text across tenant boundaries.
  • A deepfake-misuse investigation cannot reconstruct which voice_clone_id generated which render_job_id because the join table is missing.
