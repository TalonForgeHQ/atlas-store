Monica Multi-Model AI Assistant Audit Checklist

By Atlas (Talon Forge) · Lead 744 · Updated 2026-07-20

Long-tail target: Monica multi-model AI assistant audit, with supporting searches for Monica model routing audit, Monica browser extension compliance, Monica human-oversight evidence, and Monica retention/deletion audit.

Monica's current first-party homepage describes an all-in-one assistant powered by GPT-5.5, Claude 4.7 Opus, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT Image 2, Sora 2, Nano Banana 2, and other models. The product spans chat, search, writing, document summaries, translation, code generation, image creation, and video generation. That breadth makes a static privacy-policy review insufficient: a buyer needs an event-level chain that shows which workflow, model, provider, human decision, and data policy applied to each output.

Gap 1 — Request-to-model provenance

For each request, preserve the source context, account or tenant, product surface, selected model/provider, model version, prompt or system-policy version, output hash, timestamp, and region. If routing is automatic, record the routing rule and reason rather than only the model name.

  • Request ID and source-context hash
  • Selected provider, model, and version
  • Routing rule or user selection
  • Prompt/system-policy version
  • Generated-output hash and timestamp

Gap 2 — Browser and app data-use evidence

A multi-surface assistant should connect each browser or app action to the permission used, data category accessed, stated purpose, retention window, and downstream provider. The evidence should distinguish user-selected text, page context, uploaded files, account history, and generated media rather than grouping everything as generic usage data.

  • Surface and permission used
  • Data category and purpose
  • Tenant, role, locale, and region
  • Downstream provider and transfer path
  • Retention and training-use setting

Gap 3 — Human review and downstream use

Generated replies, summaries, code, images, and video need a reconstruction path from draft to human decision. A buyer should be able to determine whether the user accepted, edited, rejected, copied, downloaded, or sent the result, including the destination where the product controls that action.

  • Draft output and model/provider lineage
  • Accept, edit, reject, copy, download, or send event
  • Final-output hash after user edits
  • Destination surface or export type
  • Override, warning, or escalation event

Gap 4 — Model-catalog and provider change management

When Monica adds, removes, renames, or changes a model or provider, the audit record should identify affected workflows, policy changes, customer notice, rollback criteria, and remediation. This turns a changing homepage model catalog into an operational control rather than an undocumented dependency.

  • Old and new catalog versions
  • Affected features and tenants
  • Provider and policy deltas
  • Customer notice and effective date
  • Rollback, exception, and remediation receipt

Gap 5 — Access, retention, and deletion cascade

Monica's first-party privacy policy provides contact@monica.im for rights requests. A procurement-grade pack should go further by showing how a request travels from Monica-controlled stores to provider-held data, caches, logs, generated media, backups, and legally retained exceptions.

  • Request identity and scope
  • Monica-controlled store inventory
  • Provider cascade and completion status
  • Backup or legal-retention exception
  • Completion timestamp and customer receipt

Minimum export schema

A compact export can join: request_id, tenant_id, surface, source_context_hash, provider, model_version, routing_rule_version, prompt_policy_version, output_hash, human_decision, final_output_hash, destination, region, retention_policy_version, deletion_request_id, and completion_receipt.

How buyers use this checklist

Security, privacy, legal, and procurement teams can map these five joins to GDPR and UK GDPR accountability, SOC 2 change-management and access controls, NIST AI RMF governance, ISO/IEC 42001 AI-management controls, and applicable EU AI Act transparency and human-oversight obligations. The checklist does not assume Monica's legal parent, registered jurisdiction, certifications, representative appointments, or product-risk classification; those remain evidence requests until verified.
