1. Why this checklist exists (the audit-evidence problem no single vendor solves)

Every production AI agent + LLM application deployed at a regulated enterprise today inherits at least 4 audit-evidence surfaces simultaneously: (a) SOC 2 CC7.2 — the operational-component "system operations" criterion that requires monitoring of system components + detection of failures + response to incidents; (b) EU AI Act Art. 12 — record-keeping obligations for high-risk AI systems requiring automatic logging of events over the system's lifecycle; (c) ISO 42001 §9.4 — AI risk management performance evaluation + monitoring + measurement; (d) OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt injection) + LLM06 (sensitive information disclosure) — the most-cited vulnerabilities in production LLM applications per OWASP's 2025 refresh. None of these frameworks is independent; an auditor probing SOC 2 CC7.2 will pull threads into EU AI Act Art. 12 logging requirements, and an EU AI Act Annex III §4 high-risk classification triggers ISO 42001 + OWASP LLM Top 10 + GDPR Art. 22 automated-decision-making + Illinois BIPA (if biometric) + HIPAA (if PHI) + FedRAMP Moderate (if federal) all at once.

The hard truth: no single AI agent platform vendor supplies the full audit-evidence surface. AgentOps supplies LLM-call-chain replay. Helicone supplies the LLM-gateway chokepoint. Langfuse supplies open-source LLM-observability. Arize Phoenix supplies OpenInference-OpenTelemetry-GenAI spans. LangChain supplies the upstream agent framework + LangSmith observability. CrewAI supplies the multi-agent orchestration. AutoGen supplies the Microsoft Research multi-agent conversation. LlamaIndex supplies the RAG framework + retrieval-source-citation. Vellum supplies the prompt-engineering-workspace + LLM-deployment-control-plane. Sierra supplies the customer-facing-agent frontline. Voiceflow supplies the agent-flow-diagram-version-control design platform. Each one gives you a slice. The auditor wants the joined audit-trail — every prompt + every LLM call + every tool call + every retrieval + every multi-agent handoff + every downstream state change reconstructible from a single AI-action-id. That join-table is what this checklist demands.

2. The 11-question buyer checklist (audit-prep audit)

Required join-table: ai_action_id → prompt_template_version_hash + llm_call_chain_hash + model_version_at_evaluation_hash + tool_call_chain_hash + retrieval_source_citation_hash + multi_agent_handoff_hash + human_in_the_loop_interjection_hash + guardrail_trigger_decision_hash + downstream_state_change_hash + cost_usd_total + latency_ms_total + user_id_hash + regulatory_retain_until + compliance_metadata_chain. Retention: 7-year WORM. Drill cadence: quarterly reconstruction drill on the regulated-enterprise customer stack.

Frameworks: SOC 2 CC7.2 (system operations monitoring) + EU AI Act Art. 12 (record-keeping for high-risk AI systems) + ISO 42001 §9.4 (performance evaluation + monitoring) + OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt injection detection) + OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM06 (sensitive information disclosure detection).

Question 2 — Prompt-injection + jailbreak detection evidence

Auditor fingerprint: "show me every inbound payload that hit your AI agent in the last 90 days, what your pre-classification layer decided, what got blocked, what got sanitized, and what got through." Required detection-log columns: inbound_payload_hash + pre_classification_sanitization_result + blocked_event_flag + sanitization_method + downstream_state_change_flag + incident_response_escalation_id + model_provider_redaction_applied + user_id_hash + detection_confidence_score + regulatory_retain_until. Without this, the AI agent fails OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 at first read.

Frameworks: OWASP LLM Top 10 LLM01 (prompt injection) + ISO 42001 §6.1.4 (AI risk treatment) + NIST AI RMF MEASURE (AI risk measurement) + EU AI Act Art. 14 (human oversight) + EU AI Act Art. 15 (accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity).
