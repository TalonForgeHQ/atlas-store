3. Five-gap checklist

3.1 Identity inventory provenance

Bind agent, application, environment, API key, OAuth app, service account, owner, creation timestamp, last-use timestamp, scope, region, and revocation event. The evidence packet should make stale, orphaned, and over-scoped identities queryable.

3.2 Least-privilege approval chain

Reconstruct requested scope → risk classification → policy version → human approver → expiry or rotation → selected identity → permitted tool call. Record denied calls and policy exceptions beside successful calls.

3.3 Tenant and environment isolation

Test that customer A cannot retrieve customer B's identity metadata, secrets, policy decisions, embeddings, or audit events. Separate production, staging, development, and regional boundaries, and document the test result and cleanup receipt.

3.4 Runtime action accountability

For an agent-driven action, join the instruction or workflow run to the identity selected, credential version, OAuth scope, API request, downstream state change, human approval, rollback target, and incident escalation. This is the audit trail that procurement teams can replay.

3.5 Retention, deletion, and oversight

Show export shape, retention window, deletion cascade, legal-hold override, and sub-processor boundary for identity and action records. Where a human reviews or blocks a consequential agent action, capture reviewer identity, decision, timestamp, reason code, and rollback state for an EU AI Act Art. 14(4) evidence binder.
