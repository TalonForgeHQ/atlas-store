2014
Ironclad is founded
Attorney Jason Boehmig and engineer Cai Wangwilt team up to bring contracts into the digital age. What begins as Ironclad.ai is built to be simple, flexible, and open — giving legal and business teams a better way to power the world’s contracts.
2015
First office opens in San Francisco
Launched out of Y Combinator, Ironclad opens its first headquarters in San Francisco’s South of Market (SOMA) district, with the early goal of becoming “the best CLM in SOMA”.
2017
Series A and product launch
Ironclad announces its Series A, led by Accel, and unveils its intelligent contract management platform to help legal teams automate and streamline the contracting process.
2019
Series B & C, NYC office, and Apex debuts
Ironclad raises its Series B (led by Sequoia Capital) and Series C (led by Y Combinator). Headcount grows by 220%, a New York office opens, and the company launches Apex, its first summit for in-house legal leaders — laying the foundation for today’s thriving Ironclad community.
2020
First Docx-native CLM launches
Ironclad releases the world’s first Docx-native editor, bridging Microsoft Word contracting workflows with digital contracting.
2021
Series D, PactSafe acquisition, and Smart Import
Ironclad raises Series D, acquires clickwrap pioneer PactSafe, and launches Smart Import, an AI-powered tool to upload, tag, and index contracts — making them searchable and surfacing key insights from contract data.
2022
Ironclad AI debuts, Series E raised, 1B contracts processed
Ironclad introduces Ironclad AI to power contract generation, data extraction, and analytics across the full lifecycle. The company also raises Series E funding and surpasses one billion contracts processed.
2023
AI Assist launches
Ironclad launches AI Assist, a GPT-4–powered contract negotiation tool that flags risk, drafts clauses, takes a first pass at redlining, helping legal teams move faster with confidence.
2024
eSignature and agentic AI assistant, Jurist
Ironclad releases a native eSignature experience and introduces Jurist, a purpose-built AI assistant that drafts, edits, summarizes, translates, and explains contracts — with transparent reasoning behind every action.
2025
Dan Springer named CEO, 2 billion contracts processed
Dan Springer, former CEO of Docusign and Responsys, joins as CEO. Ironclad also crosses two billion contracts processed on its platform.

Dan Springer

Chief Executive Officer

Springer joined Ironclad as Chief Executive Officer (CEO) in May of 2025 and is excited to deliver our vision of redefining how companies manage their contracts. He also serves on the boards of UiPath and SmartNews. Springer has over 25 years of executive leadership experience in driving innovation and hypergrowth across technology, specifically SaaS, companies. He helmed both DocuSign and Responsys as CEO, taking the two companies public and transforming them into dramatic leaders in their spaces. Springer has also previously served as a board member at both public and private companies, including iCIMS, Ansira, YuMe, ELOAN, Heighten, Persado, and eGroups (Yahoo!), as well as at nonprofits, including The Boys and Girls Club, YearUp, The Urban School, Shop.org, AdTech, The Randall Museum and The San Francisco Friends School. Springer holds an MBA from Harvard University and an AB in Mathematics and Economics from Occidental College and has been honored as both a Bay Area Most Admired CEO and Best CEO.
