The Art. 14 human-oversight evidence chain for Gong

Article 14 requires documented human oversight at every point where an AI system materially influences a downstream decision. For a conversation-intelligence vendor like Gong, that means at minimum four logged checkpoints per call:

  1. Call-recording → transcription (consent disclosure + rep-side recording indicator + EU caller notification per GDPR Art. 13)
  2. Transcription → AI summary (logged model version + confidence threshold + rep-edited vs auto-routed toggle)
  3. AI summary → CRM state-change (deal stage auto-advance requires rep-approval per Art. 14; full audit log in Salesforce/HubSpot)
  4. AI summary → rep coaching nudge (per-rep review-of-nudge documented for performance-evaluation Art. 22 implication)

Most pre-2024 platforms skip checkpoint 3 — the AI summary auto-routes to CRM with no logged human approval. This is the single most common vendor-DD gap we see across conversation-intelligence vendors in 2026 audits.
