**Subject:** 5 audit questions for Rippling's HR, IT, and Finance automation

Hi Parker,

Rippling connects employee identity, payroll, benefits, devices, apps, spend, and global workforce operations in one system. As AI and workflow automation cross those products, the enterprise diligence question becomes unusually concrete: can a buyer reconstruct why an automated decision was made, which employee and company data informed it, who or what approved it, and what changed downstream?

I run a focused **$500 / 48-hour audit** for production AI and automation platforms. These are the five questions I would test on one Rippling employee-event-to-system-action route:

1. **End-to-end provenance:** Can one export join company, employee, role, source event, policy and workflow version, model or rules-engine version, retrieved record, decision, approval, API or connector call, resulting payroll/benefits/device/app/spend mutation, timestamp, and attributed cost?
2. **Authority and human approval:** Which high-impact actions—pay changes, termination workflows, benefits enrollment, device lockdown, app access, card controls, or international employment changes—require explicit human approval, and can Rippling prove the approver had the right authority at that moment?
3. **Tenant, identity, and secret isolation:** Can customers retrieve evidence that employee records, payroll data, health and benefits fields, device telemetry, financial data, connector credentials, support access, and regional processing remain isolated across companies, subsidiaries, workspaces, and jurisdictions?
4. **Hostile input and change control:** What prevents poisoned documents, manipulated employee records, prompt injection, compromised integrations, or unsafe workflow changes from steering automated decisions—and are policies, prompts, models, mappings, and connector scopes versioned with tested rollback?
5. **Incident, retention, and deletion reconstruction:** After an incorrect pay run, unauthorized access change, leaked employee record, compromised connector, discriminatory outcome, or deletion request, can Rippling identify every affected action, preserve WORM-capable evidence, and prove correction or deletion across operational stores, caches, analytics, exports, and backups?
