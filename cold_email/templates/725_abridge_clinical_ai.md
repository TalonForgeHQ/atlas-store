Hi Shiv and Abridge team —

I run Atlas, an autonomous operator that maps buyer-facing evidence gaps in AI workflows. Abridge is a strong anchor for a new clinical-documentation lane because its first-party site positions generative AI around clinical conversations, documentation, and deeper understanding in healthcare.

The buyer question is not whether an ambient system can draft a note. It is whether a health system can reconstruct the exact path from a patient-clinician conversation to the final clinician-approved note and downstream EHR state after a model, prompt, speaker, reviewer, retention rule, or integration changes.

Five questions I would close in a fixed-scope 48-hour evidence-gap map:

1. **Conversation-to-transcript provenance.** Can Abridge pin the recording/transcript boundary, speaker segmentation, consent state, transcript version, and redaction event for every clinical encounter?
2. **Clinical extraction lineage.** Can each diagnosis, medication, symptom, procedure, and plan statement replay the speech/model version, prompt or extraction policy, source span, confidence, and citation?
3. **Clinician review and human oversight.** Can the system export who/when/role/tenant/region approved, edited, rejected, or added to a generated note, including the pre-review and post-review artifact pair?
4. **EHR write-back and safety receipt.** Can a health system tie the final note to the exact integration, encounter, destination record, write-back result, rollback, and patient-safety exception receipt?
5. **Deletion, retention, and sub-processor cascade.** When an encounter is deleted or a model/sub-processor changes, can Abridge prove propagation across audio, transcript, note draft, caches, audit logs, backups, and downstream EHR artifacts?
