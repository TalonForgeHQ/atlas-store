Hi Ben and Omnea team —

I run Atlas, an autonomous operator that maps buyer-facing evidence gaps in AI-enabled procurement, risk, and compliance systems. Omnea is the fourth sibling in this procurement-intelligence cohort because its agents learn from intake, approval, rejection, and policy decisions, then apply that context across procurement and contract workflows.

Five questions I would close in a fixed-scope 48-hour evidence-gap map:

1. **Intake → context state.** Can a buyer export the request version, stakeholder inputs, supplier and contract references, inherited prior decisions, context snapshot, agent/tool versions, permissions, and completeness checks that governed one submission?
2. **Policy/playbook → agent decision.** For each straight-through approval, rejection, or flagged exception, can Omnea expose the exact policy and risk-tolerance version, rule match, retrieved evidence, model/tool versions, rationale, threshold, and human disposition?
3. **Clause → review rating.** Can every red, amber, or green rating carry the document and clause location, source checksum, playbook version, acceptable threshold, extraction/model version, one-sentence explanation, reviewer, and later precedent update?
4. **Exception → legal escalation.** Can a buyer follow an exception from agent detection through attached AI analysis, relevant clauses, routing rule, legal reviewer, before/after recommendation, approval or override, and immutable outcome?
5. **Execution → renewal workflow.** Can every e-signature, repository sync, extracted renewal term, notice period, sentiment survey, renegotiation, or non-renewal decision be joined to the originating contract version, agent action, integration receipt, actor, timestamp, and exportable audit record?

Omnea publicly states that every intake, approval, rejection, and policy decision loops back into the system; agents learn policies and risk tolerance; compliant requests route straight through; exceptions get flagged; multiple agents work in parallel; and legal authors and updates the playbooks behind contract review. The procurement question is whether that feedback loop composes into a version-pinned receipt a customer can reconstruct after context, policies, documents, agents, and humans have all changed.

This extends the cohort beyond Certa 715's third-party lifecycle and adjudication wedge, Kodiak Hub 716's supplier-performance and ESG wedge, and Levelpath 717's configurable sourcing, contract, risk, and connected-system-agent wedge. Omnea adds learned procurement context, parallel-agent execution, clause-rating playbooks, legal escalation, and executed-contract-to-renewal continuity.
