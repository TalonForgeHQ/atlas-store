## Question 1 — Behavioral model training data + labeling
We have the procurement-side buyers on every enterprise RFP asking the same question:
"Where does your training data for the per-identity behavioral baseline come from, how is it versioned, when was it last refreshed, and what's the per-tenant isolation boundary between Abnormal's foundation model and customer-specific tuning?"
Buyers will read your public Trust Center language and (rightly) ask for a vendor letter that says something like:
"(a) all customer data used for per-tenant baseline training is logically isolated and never contributes to other tenants' baselines; (b) the Attune model card describes the inclusion lists and exclusion lists of data types; (c) customer-Admin may opt out of behavioral baseline training by tenant or mailbox; (d) the validation set / confusion matrix for false-positive rate is published per model version."
Without this packet Abnormal loses to Proofpoint + Mimecast + Microsoft on every regulated procurement — not on detection quality, but on attestation.

## Question 2 — AI-Automation human-oversight + EU AI Act Art. 14
The autonomous AI-Security-Mailbox ("AI co-worker for email security operations") and AI-Data-Analyst ("Ask plain-English questions, get security insights instantly") surfaces are explicit. EU AI Act Article 14(4) requires that a human-oversight step be operationally captured per-output for any GPAI-deployed system that materially affects a natural person. Regulated European buyers are now asking: "Where in the audit log is the per-tenant per-action evidence of the human-oversight step (or its approved absence)?" Buyers want to see, for each Autonomous-Remediation action:
- triggered input
- recommended action + confidence
- approver identity + timestamp + role
- auto-remediated result + downstream notify / quarantine / delete / hold / restore fingerprint
- rollback capability + retention

## Question 4 — Detection 360 + Threat Log retention + customer export
Your product page describes "Detection 360 — see exactly why any email was blocked or delivered" and "Threat Log — full visibility into every blocked threat with behavioral context." Regulated buyers will want:
(a) per-decision retention window (line with the GDPR Art. 5(1)(e) storage-limitation principle — most regulated buyers want 365 days minimum + extendable + documented deletion),

(c) deletion cascade: when a tenant cancels, when tenant-data is purged (with optional legal-hold override + provable purge log).
